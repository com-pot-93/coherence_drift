The process starts with reporting a vacancy to HR. Then, a decision is made; if it's true, a job description is created. Subsequently, a series of checks are performed to determine if clarification is needed.

The process enters a loop where, depending on whether clarification is needed, the department is asked for clarification. This loop can repeat multiple times through several exclusive gateways. Each time, a decision is made whether to ask for clarification again or to proceed if no further clarification is needed.

Once all necessary clarifications are obtained, the process continues to another decision point. If a condition is met, the job description is submitted. Following submission, another series of checks are performed to determine if corrections are needed.

If corrections are needed, the job description is corrected, and the process loops back to the "Create Job Description" task. If no correction is needed, a decision is made based on whether a condition is met. If the condition is met, the job description is submitted again. If the condition is not met, the job is posted.

After posting the job, there are several exclusive gateways that lead back to the "Post Job" task, creating a loop. This loop can be exited through a series of exclusive gateways.

Finally, there is an escalation event that leads back to the initial decision point after reporting the vacancy to HR, creating a loop in the process. The process ends when all conditions are met and the end event is reached.
