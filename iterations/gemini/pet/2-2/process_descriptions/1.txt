The process starts with receiving customer data, followed by entering this data into a CRM system. The data is then compared and checked for accuracy. If the data is not correct, it is corrected, otherwise, the process proceeds directly to the next step. Next, a decision is made whether to create a new customer object. If the answer is yes, a new customer object is created; otherwise, this step is skipped. Following this, contract documents are prepared and then checked. If there are issues with the documents, they are analyzed and resolved; otherwise, the process proceeds directly to the next step. A confirmation and request are then sent, and the request is checked. If there is a conflict, suppliers are informed, and demand resolution is initiated; otherwise, this step is skipped. The agreement is then communicated and terminated. A decision is then made whether to create a CIS contract. If the answer is yes, a CIS contract is created; otherwise, this step is skipped. The contract details are then checked, and finally, meter data is received, which concludes the process.
