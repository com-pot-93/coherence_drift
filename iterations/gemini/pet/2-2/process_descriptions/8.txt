The process starts when customer data is received. This data is then entered into a CRM system, after which its accuracy is compared against a reference. If the data is not accurate, it is corrected. Otherwise, if the data is accurate or after it has been corrected, a decision is made whether to create a customer object. If the decision is positive, the customer object is created. Regardless of whether the customer object is created or not, contract documents are prepared and subsequently checked for issues. If issues are found, they are analyzed and resolved. Otherwise, if no issues are found or after the issues have been resolved, a confirmation and request are sent. The request is then checked, and if it is confirmed, suppliers are informed, and demand resolution is initiated. If the request is not confirmed or after the demand resolution is initiated, the agreement is communicated and terminated. Following this, a decision is made whether to create a CIS contract. If the decision is positive, the CIS contract is created. Regardless of whether the CIS contract is created or not, the contract details are checked, and meter data is received, which concludes the process.
