The process starts and immediately encounters a decision point.

If Condition A is met, the process receives an expense report and then encounters another decision point. If Condition E is met, a new employee account is created, followed by a parallel execution that merges back together, and then proceeds to another merge point. If Condition E is not met, the process skips the account creation and goes directly to the merge point.

If Condition A is not met, the process skips the expense report reception and directly creates a new employee account, followed by a parallel execution that merges back together, and then proceeds to a merge point.

From the merge point, the process encounters a decision point. If Condition G is met, the process encounters another decision point. If Condition G is met again, the report is automatically approved, and the process continues to a merge point. If Condition G is not met, the supervisor approves the report, and the process continues to the merge point.

From the merge point, the process encounters another merge point. If Condition I is met, reimbursement is sent to the bank account, and the process continues to a merge point. If Condition I is not met, the process skips the reimbursement and goes directly to the merge point.

From the merge point, the process encounters a decision point. If Condition K is met, the process skips to a merge point. Otherwise, the process encounters another decision point. If Condition L is met, an approval in progress email is sent, and the process continues to a merge point. Otherwise, the process encounters another decision point. If Condition M is not met, a rejection notice is sent, and the process continues to a merge point. If Condition M is met, the process skips the rejection notice and goes directly to the merge point.

From the merge point, the process continues to another merge point, and then to the merge point.

Finally, the process ends.
