The process starts with notifying a customer, followed by inquiring about patient data and informing the nursing officer. Next, a full patient inquiry is conducted, and the information is recorded. The registration form is then handed in, and the form information is stored. After that, an information form is printed.

A decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," a patient file is created, the registration form is stored, registration cards are produced, and a new patient is added. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next step.

Then, a staff meeting is held, new patients are assigned, and the assignments are recorded. The medical file is determined, and information is added to the assignment list. The assignments are stored, and the registration cards are passed on.

Another decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," a letter is prepared and sent, a copy of the medical file is received, the intaker is informed, and a copy is added to the patient file. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next step.

Next, a meeting is planned, the patient is examined, and observations are registered. The notes are copied to the file, and a checklist is added to the file.

Another decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," the first meeting is planned, tapes are typed, and information is added to the patient file. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next step.

Finally, the patient is added to a list, the list is provided to the team leader, and a treatment plan is formulated, concluding the process.
