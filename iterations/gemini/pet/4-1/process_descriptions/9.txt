The process starts with a notification to the customer, followed by an inquiry about patient data. Next, the nursing officer is informed, leading to a full patient inquiry. The information is then recorded, and a registration form is handed in. The form information is stored, and an information form is printed.

A decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," a patient file is created, the registration form is stored, registration cards are produced, and a new patient is added. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next decision point.

Another decision is made. Regardless of the previous choice, a staff meeting is held, new patients are assigned, and these assignments are recorded. The medical file is determined, and information is added to the assignment list. The assignments are then stored, and registration cards are passed on.

Another decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," a letter is prepared and sent, a copy of the medical file is received, the intaker is informed, and a copy is added to the patient file. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next decision point.

Regardless of the previous choice, a meeting is planned, the patient is examined, and observations are registered. Notes are copied to the file, and a checklist is added to the file.

Another decision is made: if the answer is "Yes," the first meeting is planned, tapes are typed, and information is added to the patient file. If the answer is "No," the process skips directly to the next task.

Regardless of the previous choice, the patient is added to a list, and this list is provided to the team leader, who formulates a treatment plan. The process then ends.
