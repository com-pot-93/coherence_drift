The process starts when the GO requests measurements from the MSP. The MSP then checks the received request. Based on the check, the process follows one of two paths. If the request is denied, the MSP denies the request. Otherwise, the MSP performs the measurement. After the measurement, the process again follows one of two paths depending on whether the measurement was a success or a failure. In case of failure, the MSP informs the GO about the failure. If the measurement was successful, the MSP transmits the measured values to the GO, which then processes the values and sends the changed values back to the MSP. Following this, the GO transmits the readings to the SP. Regardless of whether the measurement failed or succeeded, the process converges, and then merges again with the path where the request was denied. Finally, the process ends.
