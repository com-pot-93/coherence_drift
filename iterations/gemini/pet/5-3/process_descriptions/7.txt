The process starts with submitting an office supply request, which is then registered. Depending on whether Condition A is met, the process proceeds differently.

If Condition A is met, the process continues to another decision point. If the condition at this point is true, an approval process is initiated. Following the approval process, there's a decision point. If the request is not approved, comments are reviewed, and the request is resubmitted for approval. If the request is approved, quotations are requested.

After requesting quotations, the process waits for vendor selection, then generates a purchase order, waits for product delivery, and receives an invoice. A notification is then sent to the user.

Following the notification, a check is performed to see if Condition B is met. If Condition B is not met, an escalation event is triggered, and the process loops back to the decision point after the escalation event. If Condition B is met, the process loops back to requesting quotations.

If, at the initial decision point, Condition A is not met, the process ends. Also, if the approval process results in a negative decision, an escalation event is triggered, and the process loops back to the decision point after the initial registration.
