The process starts when an office supply request is submitted. The process then splits into two alternative paths.
In the first path, the process ends immediately.
In the second path, the request is registered. Then, the process splits into two alternative paths again.
If Condition A is not met, the process escalates and then registers the request again. If Condition A is met, an approval process is initiated.
The approval process can either be approved or not approved. If not approved, comments are reviewed, and the approval process is initiated again. If approved, quotations are requested.
After quotations are requested, the process splits into two parallel paths.
In the first parallel path, the process waits for vendor selection, generates a purchase order, waits for product delivery, receives an invoice, and sends a notification to the user. After the notification, the process splits into two alternative paths based on a condition. If the condition is false, the process escalates. If the condition is true, the process continues.
In the second parallel path, the process waits for vendor selection. Then, the process splits into two alternative paths based on Condition B. If Condition B is not met, the process escalates. The escalation splits into two alternative paths based on a condition. If the condition is false, the process escalates again. If the condition is true, the process continues. If Condition B is met, quotations are requested.
After both parallel paths are complete, the process continues.
Finally, the process escalates and loops back to the beginning, splitting into two alternative paths.
