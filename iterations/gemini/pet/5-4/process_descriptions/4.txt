The process starts with purchasing a product, followed by submitting an expense report. A decision is then made: if the report requires supervisor review, it is sent for review; otherwise, the process skips directly to report approval. After the supervisor review, the report is approved. Following approval, the process splits into two parallel activities: one path immediately merges back, while the other involves checking receipts. Based on the receipt check, there are three possible outcomes: the report can be sent back for editing, escalated, or the payment can be confirmed. Regardless of the outcome, the process merges back to a single flow. Finally, the process escalates and ends.
