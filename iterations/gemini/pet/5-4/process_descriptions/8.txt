The process starts with the initiation of purchasing. Subsequently, an expense report is submitted. A decision is then made: if the report requires review, it is sent for supervisor review; otherwise, the process skips directly to the report approval. After the supervisor's review, the report is approved. Following approval, the process splits into two parallel paths. The first path involves an escalation task. Simultaneously, the second path involves checking receipts, which leads to another decision point. Depending on the outcome, the process can either proceed with editing the report, escalating the report, or confirming payment. After any of these actions, the second parallel path rejoins the first. Finally, another escalation task is performed before the process ends.
