The process starts with the initiation of purchasing. Subsequently, an expense report is submitted. A decision is then made: if the report requires review, it undergoes a supervisor review; otherwise, this step is skipped. Following either the supervisor review or the skipping of it, the report is approved. After the report approval, two parallel paths are initiated: one involves an escalation task, and the other involves checking receipts. During the receipt check, a decision is made: the process can either proceed with editing the report, escalating the report, or confirming payment. After any of these three options, the process merges back to a single flow. Once both the escalation task and the receipt check (including editing, escalating, or confirming payment) are completed, an escalation task is performed, and the process concludes.
