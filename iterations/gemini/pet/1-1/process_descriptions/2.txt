The process starts. A decision is made: either the order is rejected, or the process continues. If the order is rejected, the process proceeds to the next decision point. Otherwise, two activities occur in parallel: preparing for bike assembly and processing the part list. After the part list is processed, another decision is made: either the parts are available, or they are not. If the parts are available, they are reserved. If the parts are not available, they are back-ordered. After either reserving or back-ordering the parts, the process waits for both the parts handling and the preparation for bike assembly to complete. Then, the bicycle is assembled and shipped. Finally, another decision is made before the process ends. The process ends.
