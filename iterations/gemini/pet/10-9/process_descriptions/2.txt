The process starts. It then proceeds based on one of two exclusive conditions. Either a Service Provider, Public Utility, or Government Organization requests changes to the MPO, or the MPO causes a change.

If a request for change is initiated, the process continues with a review of the change request. Alternatively, if the MPO causes a change, the process also continues with a review of the change request.

Following the review, the process diverges again based on whether the MPO rejects the change or confirms the change.

If the MPO rejects the change, the process continues by rejecting the change. Alternatively, if the MPO confirms the change, the process continues by confirming the change.

After either rejecting or confirming the change, the process continues by performing the change.

Following the execution of the change, the process diverges based on whether the MPO reports the implementation or notifies about the failure.

If the MPO reports the implementation, the process continues by reporting the implementation. Alternatively, if the MPO notifies about the failure, the process continues by notifying about the failure.

Finally, after either reporting the implementation or notifying about the failure, the process ends.
