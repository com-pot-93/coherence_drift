The process starts. It then splits into two alternative paths.

In the first path, the process splits into four alternative branches.
- In the first branch, a metering point operations bill is sent to the GO.
- In the second branch, a measurement bill is sent to the GO.
- In the third branch, a bill for additional readings is sent to the GO.
- In the fourth branch, a bill for the equipment acquisition is sent to the GO.
After any of the four branches, the process continues to examine the bill.
The process then splits based on whether the GO rejects or confirms the bill.
- If the GO rejects the bill, the invoice is rejected.
- If the GO confirms the bill, the invoice is confirmed with payment advice.
After either rejection or confirmation, the process merges back.

In the second path, a bill for equipment acquisition is sent to MPON. The bill is then examined.
The process then splits based on whether the MPON confirms or rejects the bill.
- If the MPON confirms the bill, the invoice is confirmed with payment advice.
- If the MPON rejects the bill, the invoice is rejected.
After either confirmation or rejection, the process merges back.

Finally, the two paths merge, and the process ends.
