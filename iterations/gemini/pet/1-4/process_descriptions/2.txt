The process starts. Then, a decision is made. If the condition is met, the process continues with selecting underwriters, preparing a registration statement, and checking issue compliance. After that, the process splits into two parallel activities: firming up the issue price and arranging a road show. Once both activities are completed, the process merges back into a single flow. Next, the process involves receiving SEC clearance, fixing the issue price and entering a firm commitment, and offering stock to the public. Finally, another decision is made, and the process ends. If the condition is not met at the first decision point, the process skips the activities mentioned above and proceeds directly to the final decision point before ending.
