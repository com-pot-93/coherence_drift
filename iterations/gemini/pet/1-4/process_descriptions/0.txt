The process begins when a company decides to go public. Subsequently, the company selects the underwriters, who then prepare a registration statement for the SEC's approval and check the issue's compliance. Following the compliance check, two parallel activities occur simultaneously: the underwriters begin to firm up the issue price and arrange a road show, and the underwriters await approval of the registration statement. Once both activities are completed, the underwriters receive clearance from the SEC. Next, the underwriters fix the issue price and enter into a firm commitment to buy the stock. Finally, the underwriters offer the stock to the public, concluding the process.
