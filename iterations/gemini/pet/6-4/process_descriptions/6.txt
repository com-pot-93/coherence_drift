The process starts. First, contact details are determined. Then, a decision is made based on whether it is a purchase or a known person.

If it is a purchase, the process proceeds directly to calling the customer. If it is a known person, the interested person is identified before calling the customer.

After calling the customer, the contact person and budget are determined, and the customer's interest is assessed. Following this, the process splits into two parallel paths.

In the first path, a decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation. After either presentation, the process waits for the customer.

In the second parallel path, a similar decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation. After either presentation, the process waits for the customer.

Once both paths have completed waiting for the customer, the process merges back into a single flow.

Next, a decision is made between making a sales representative call or making another sales representative call. After either call, a quotation is created.

The process then ends.
