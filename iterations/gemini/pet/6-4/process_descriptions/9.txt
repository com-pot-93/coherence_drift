The process starts. First, contact details are determined. Then, a decision is made based on whether it is a purchase or a known person. If it is a purchase, the process continues directly. If it is a known person, the interested person is identified. Regardless of the path taken, the process continues with calling the customer. Next, the contact person and budget are determined. The customer's interest is then assessed. Following this, the process splits into two parallel paths.

In the first parallel path, a decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation. After either presentation, the process waits for the customer.

In the second parallel path, a similar decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation. After either presentation, the process waits for the customer.

Once both paths are complete, the process merges back into a single flow. A decision is then made between making a sales representative call or making another sales representative call. After either call, a quotation is created. The process then ends.
