The process starts. First, contact details are determined. Then, a decision is made based on whether it is a purchase or a known person.

- If it is a purchase, the process continues directly to calling the customer.
- If the person is known, the interested person is identified before calling the customer.

After calling the customer, the contact person and budget are determined, and the customer's interest is assessed. Following this, the process splits into two parallel paths.

In the first path, a decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation.

- If a sales representative presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.
- Alternatively, if a pre-sales presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.

In the second parallel path, a similar decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation.

- If a sales representative presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.
- Alternatively, if a pre-sales presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.

After both paths have completed and the process has waited for the customer in both, the process merges back together. A decision is then made between making a sales representative call or making another sales representative call.

- If a sales representative call is made, the process continues to creating a quotation.
- If another sales representative call is made, the process continues to creating a quotation.

Finally, after the quotation is created, the process ends.
