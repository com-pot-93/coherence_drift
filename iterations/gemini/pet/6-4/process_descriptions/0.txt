The process starts when the company determines the contact details of potential customers. Then, based on certain conditions, the process follows one of three paths: the company buys details for cold calls, marketing staff participates in exhibitions, or the company already knows a person interested in the product.

After any of these options, the call center staff calls the customer, determines the contact person, determines the budget, and assesses customer interest.

Next, the process diverges based on certain conditions. It can either proceed with the head of development acquiring the customer, or directly to the next set of activities.

If the head of development acquires the customer, then, based on certain conditions, either a sales representative or a pre-sales employee makes an online presentation. After the presentation, the process waits for the customer.

Following this, based on certain conditions, either a sales representative calls the customer, or the process loops back for the sales representative to call the customer again. After the call, the company creates a quotation.

Alternatively, if the process did not involve the head of development, then, based on certain conditions, either a sales representative or a pre-sales employee makes an online presentation. After the presentation, the process waits for the customer.

Following this, based on certain conditions, either a sales representative calls the customer, or the process loops back for the sales representative to call the customer again. After the call, the company creates a quotation.

After the quotation is created, the process ends.
