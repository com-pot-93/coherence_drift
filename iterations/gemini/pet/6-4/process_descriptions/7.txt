The process starts with determining contact details. Then, a decision is made based on whether it is a purchase or a known person.

- If it is a purchase, the process proceeds directly to calling the customer.
- If it is a known person, the interested person is identified before calling the customer.

After calling the customer, the contact person and budget are determined, and then the customer's interest is assessed. Following the assessment, the process splits into two parallel paths.

In the first path, a decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation.
- If a sales representative presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.
- If a pre-sales presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.

In the second parallel path, a similar decision is made between giving a sales representative presentation or a pre-sales presentation.
- If a sales representative presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.
- If a pre-sales presentation is given, the process waits for the customer.

Once both paths have completed and the process has waited for the customer in both paths, a decision is made between making a sales representative call or making another sales representative call.
- If a sales representative call is made, a quotation is created.
- If another sales representative call is made, a quotation is created.

Finally, after the quotation is created, the process ends.
