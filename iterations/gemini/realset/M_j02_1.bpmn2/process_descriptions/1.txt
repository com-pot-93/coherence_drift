The process starts when a customized airplane order is received. The order is then processed. Following this, two activities occur in parallel: building the airplane base model and transmitting customization requirements. Once both are completed, a decision is made. If the condition is true, the process waits for 14 days. After waiting, another decision is made. If a condition is met, a reminder is sent, and the process waits for another 14 days before proceeding. If the condition is not met, the process skips sending a reminder and proceeds directly. Regardless of whether the reminder was sent or not, the next step is to install bits and pieces, followed by controlling engines and risk factors, and then preparing for a test flight. After preparation, the test flight protocol is received and forwarded to the customer. Subsequently, a decision is made based on the shipping method. If standard shipping is selected, the airplane is shipped using the standard method. If premium shipping is selected, the airplane is shipped using the premium method. After the airplane is shipped, a confirmation email is received, and the process loops back to a previous decision point. If the condition is false, the process ends.
