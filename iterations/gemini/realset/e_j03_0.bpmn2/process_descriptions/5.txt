The process starts with initiating an assessment tool, followed by starting the tool and entering the incident type. Next, the process requires specifying the incident. Based on the incident specification, the process follows one of three possible paths: either contacting accident insurance and the principal, contacting private insurance, or entering user/victim data. After any of these three paths, the process merges back together.
The process then splits based on whether contacting superiors is required. If not, the process splits into two parallel paths: one path involves contacting accident insurance, while the other path involves a conditional split. If a certain condition is met, the labor inspectorate is contacted; otherwise, this step is skipped. After both parallel paths are completed, the process merges back together.
If contacting superiors is required, the process skips the parallel paths and proceeds directly to contacting superiors. After contacting superiors or after the merge of the parallel paths, the process proposes tasks to the user, then executes the proposed task, and finally ends.
