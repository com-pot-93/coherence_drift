The process starts with creating a parts list, followed by querying webshops to find the required parts. The cheapest shop is then chosen, and an order is placed. After placing the order, four activities occur in parallel: receiving the order, sending the invoice, delivering the order, and updating the inventory. Once all four activities are completed, the order is received again, and the building process starts.

Next, a decision is made based on whether a certain condition is met. If the condition is met, the process proceeds to another decision point. If the condition is not met, a parallel path is taken where a decision is made based on whether condition A is met or not. Regardless of whether condition A is met or not, the process continues to order faster delivery, and then proceeds to the same decision point as the first path.

At this decision point, a choice is made based on whether condition C is met. If condition C is met, another decision is made based on condition D. If condition D is met, a compliment email is written. If condition D is not met, the cheapest part is ordered. After writing the compliment email or ordering the cheapest part, the process merges back together.

If condition C is not met, a decision is made based on condition E. If condition E is met, the cheapest part is ordered. If condition E is not met, the process is escalated. After ordering the cheapest part or escalating the process, the process merges back together. Finally, the process ends.
