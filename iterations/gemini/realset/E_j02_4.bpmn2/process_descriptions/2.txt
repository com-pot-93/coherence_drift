The process starts. It then proceeds to two parallel activities: checking if the baby is younger than 2 years and checking if the person is a parent. After both checks are completed, a decision is made based on employment status.

If the person is self-employed, studying, a housewife, or a man, the process skips directly to a later decision point. If the person is employed, a notification is sent to the employer, confirmation is received, and then maternity leave is taken, before proceeding to the same later decision point.

At that decision point, the process branches into three exclusive paths based on different conditions.

In the first path, a decision is made based on whether the time is "Less than 2 months" or "More than 3 months". Depending on the outcome, a notification is sent to the employer. Then, another decision is made, branching into "Decision Path 1" or "Decision Path 2". Depending on the path, a specific task is performed. After either task, the employer notification is received, maternity leave is taken, and the process merges back.

In the second path, a decision is made based on whether the time is "Less than 2 months" or "More than 3 months". Depending on the outcome, a notification is sent to the employer. Then, another decision is made, branching into "Decision Path 1" or "Decision Path 2". Depending on the path, a specific task is performed. After either task, the employer notification is received, maternity leave is taken, and the process merges back.

In the third path, if both parents take maternity leave, the process merges directly back.

Finally, the process continues and ends.
