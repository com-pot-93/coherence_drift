The process starts. First, the required resources are determined. Then, two activities occur in parallel: checking the repository and collecting urgently needed resources. After both are completed, a decision is made. If resources are sufficient, the process proceeds directly. If not, tools are built. Following either path, the remaining resources are collected. Next, two activities occur in parallel: one path involves evaluating a condition, waiting for an event, and then, depending on whether to reset a bot or not, either resetting the bot or proceeding directly to starting farming. After farming starts, depending on whether a condition is true or false, the player is notified or not. Then, one of three actions is taken: giving a sleeping break, bragging to friends, or helping with materials. After any of these actions, the condition is evaluated again. The other parallel path involves updating the resource list. After both parallel paths are complete, a decision is made. If a condition is true, another decision is made. Depending on whether a condition is true or not, the process either waits for an event or escalates. If the condition is false, the process escalates. After escalation, the process loops back to the decision point before the escalation. If the process waits for an event, then, depending on whether to reset a bot or not, either the bot is reset or the process proceeds directly to starting farming. After farming starts, depending on whether a condition is true or false, the player is notified or not. Then, one of three actions is taken: giving a sleeping break, bragging to friends, or helping with materials. After any of these actions, the condition is evaluated again. The process then proceeds to a point where it loops back to the decision point before the escalation. Finally, the process ends.
