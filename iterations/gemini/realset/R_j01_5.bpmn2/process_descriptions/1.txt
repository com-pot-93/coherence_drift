The process starts. First, a machine is run, and then a connection is checked. If the connection check is positive, the process proceeds to a merging point. If the connection check is negative, the process branches to another merging point based on whether Condition A or Condition B is met. If Condition A is met, the process continues to the next step. If Condition B is met, the process loops back to the merging point of the connection check.

If Condition A is met, measurements are collected. Subsequently, the process either calculates the Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) or escalates to a merging point based on a condition. If the condition is met, the OEE is calculated. After calculating OEE, the process splits into two parallel activities: stopping the machine and sending an email to an engineer. After both activities are completed, the process merges. If the condition is not met, the process escalates and merges.

After the merging point, the process merges again with the positive branch of the connection check. Finally, the process ends.
