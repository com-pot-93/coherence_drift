The process starts. First, a machine is run, and then a connection is checked. Depending on the outcome of the connection check, the process either proceeds directly or branches to another decision point. If the process branches, a condition is evaluated. If the condition is met, the process proceeds; otherwise, measurements are collected before proceeding. The process then merges back to a single flow. Another condition is evaluated. If this condition is met, the process proceeds directly; otherwise, OEE is calculated. Following this, the process splits into two parallel activities: the machine is stopped, and an email is sent to an engineer. Once both activities are completed, the process merges back to a single flow. Finally, the process ends.
