The process starts. First, a machine is run, and then a connection is checked. Depending on whether the connection check is positive or negative, the process follows different paths.

If the connection check is positive, the process proceeds directly to a merging point. If the connection check is negative, the process continues to another decision point.

At this decision point, if a certain condition B is met, the process proceeds to the same merging point as the positive path. If condition B is not met, the process continues to a task where measurements are collected.

After collecting measurements, there is another decision point. If a condition is met, the process calculates the Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE). Following the OEE calculation, the process splits into two parallel paths. One path stops the machine, and the other sends an email to an engineer. After both paths are completed, they merge back together.

If the condition after collecting measurements is not met, the process proceeds directly to the merging point of the parallel paths.

Finally, the process ends.
