The process starts. First, the machine is run, and then the connection is checked. Depending on whether the connection check is positive or negative, the process proceeds differently.

If the connection check is positive, the process continues to a merging point. If the connection check is negative, a further decision is made based on whether Condition B is met. If Condition B is met, the process continues to the same merging point. If Condition B is not met, measurements are collected before proceeding to the merging point.

After the merging point, a decision is made. If a condition is met, the process proceeds to another merging point. Otherwise, the Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) is calculated. Following the OEE calculation, the process splits into two parallel activities: stopping the machine and sending an email to an engineer. After both activities are completed, the process merges again.

From the second merging point, the process continues to the final merging point. Finally, the process ends.
