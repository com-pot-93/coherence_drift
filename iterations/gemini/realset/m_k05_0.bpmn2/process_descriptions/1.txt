The process starts. First, the requirements are collected. Then, a decision is made, and based on the outcome, the process either loops back to the decision point or proceeds forward. If the process proceeds, the requirements are sent, and a draft is received. The feasibility of the draft is then checked. If it's feasible, the requirements are refined, otherwise a decision is made. If the answer is "Yes", a report for a building permit is created. Regardless of the answer, the process continues with creating an order list and placing an order. Next, the effort is calculated, and the process splits into two parallel paths. The first path involves getting the order. The second path involves a decision. If the answer is "Yes", friends are called; otherwise, a building company is hired. After calling friends or hiring a company, both paths merge back together. The process then merges back to the point where the requirements are refined. Finally, the process merges back to the point where the requirements are sent. The process merges back to the first decision point and ends.
