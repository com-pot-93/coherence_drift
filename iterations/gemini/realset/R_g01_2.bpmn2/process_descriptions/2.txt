The process starts when the manufacturer is entered, followed by entering the model.
After this, a decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes", another decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes" again, the broken part is entered.
Then, a decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes", another decision is made.
If the answer is "No", the broken part is entered again.
Additionally, an escalation event occurs after entering the broken part.
After the escalation event, a decision is made.
The process returns to the second decision point.
If the answer to the first decision is "No", the process continues to a parallel gateway.
At the parallel gateway, the process splits into two parallel activities: computing the parts list and computing the necessary tools.
After both activities are completed, the process merges back together.
The list is then checked against the inventory.
Following this, a decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes", the process continues.
If the answer is "No", a friend is contacted, and everything else needed is obtained.
After obtaining everything else, the process continues.
Instructions are then generated.
Another decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes", the process continues.
If the answer is "No", the instructions are followed.
After following the instructions, the process continues.
A loop occurs.
Another decision is made.
If the answer is "Yes", the process continues.
If the answer is "No", a review is left.
After leaving a review, the process continues.
Finally, the phone is sent to an expert, and the process ends.
