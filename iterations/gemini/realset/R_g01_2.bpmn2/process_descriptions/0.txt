The process starts when the manufacturer is entered, followed by entering the model. Then, based on a decision, the process either proceeds directly to a merging point or requires entering the broken part first. If the broken part is entered, there is a loop back to the decision point where the process either proceeds directly to a merging point or requires entering the broken part again.

After the merging point, the process splits into two parallel activities: computing the parts list and computing necessary tools. Once both are completed, the process continues to check the list against the available inventory.

Next, a decision is made. The process either proceeds directly to a merging point, or it branches out to contact a friend and then get everything else needed before reaching the merging point.

After the merging point, instructions to fix the issue are generated. Another decision is made, and the process either proceeds directly to a merging point or involves following the instructions. If the instructions are followed, there is a loop where the instructions are followed repeatedly until a decision is made to either escalate the process or proceed to a merging point.

From the merging point, the process either involves sending the phone to an expert or leaving a review. If the phone is sent to an expert, the process proceeds to a merging point. If a review is left, there is a decision to either upload a video, which is then saved to a database, or proceed directly to a merging point.

Finally, after the merging point, the process ends.
