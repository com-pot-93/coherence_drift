The process starts. A task is performed, and then a decision is made. If the decision is "Yes," a car owner is notified, and another decision is made. If this second decision is "Yes," a task is performed, and the car owner is fined, leading to a merge point. If the second decision is "No," car problems are received, payment is offered, and a third decision is made. If this third decision is "Yes," payment is transferred before reaching another merge point. If the third decision is "No," the process directly proceeds to the merge point. Both paths from the second and third decisions converge. If the initial decision is "No," the process skips the notification and subsequent tasks, proceeding directly to a merge point. From this merge point, repair confirmation is received, followed by receiving the next service time. The process then ends.
