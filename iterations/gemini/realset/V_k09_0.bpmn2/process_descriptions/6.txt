Subsequently, another decision is made, followed by another decision. If all conditions are met, the process splits into three parallel paths.

In the first path, a decision is made. If the condition is met, a delivery delay penalty is applied; otherwise, the task is marked as OK. After either applying the penalty or marking the task as OK, the process merges back.

In the second parallel path, a decision is made. If the condition is met, a delivery delay penalty is applied; otherwise, the task is marked as OK. After either applying the penalty or marking the task as OK, the process merges back.

In the third parallel path, a decision is made. If the condition is met, a delivery delay penalty is applied; otherwise, the task is marked as OK. After either applying the penalty or marking the task as OK, the process merges back.

After all three parallel paths converge, the arrived product is marked as OK. The process then splits into two parallel paths. One path involves packing the product, while the other involves requesting a courier. After both paths are completed, the delivery is marked as shipped.
