The process starts. First, a basic design is developed, followed by ordering Lego brick sets and then sorting the Lego bricks. After sorting, the process splits into three parallel activities: putting big bricks in a container, putting medium bricks in a container, and putting small bricks in a container. Once all three container tasks are completed, the process merges back into a single flow.

Next, there is a decision point. If the condition is true, the process continues to build subcomponents. After building subcomponents, there is another decision point with two possible outcomes: either build more subcomponents or reorder Lego bricks. Regardless of the path taken, the process converges.

Following this, the subcomponents are tested, leading to another decision point. Here, the process can either redesign a subcomponent or assemble the subcomponents into the machine. After either redesigning or assembling, the process merges and loops back to the initial decision point where the condition was evaluated.

If the condition is false, the process ends.
