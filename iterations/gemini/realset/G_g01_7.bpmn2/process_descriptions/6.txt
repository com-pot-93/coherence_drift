The process starts with sending invitations, followed by requesting game preferences and choosing the games. After this, the process splits into two parallel paths.

In the first path, a decision is made. If the condition is true, a LAN party date is proposed. Subsequently, another decision point is reached. This decision point loops back to proposing a LAN party date if the date needs changing. If an issue arises, the process escalates and then loops back to proposing a LAN party date. Otherwise, the process continues after the decision. The process then returns to the initial decision point.

In the second parallel path, a decision is made. If the condition is true, guests are asked about games. Another decision point is reached, which loops back to asking guests about games if the games need changing. If an issue arises, the process escalates and then loops back to asking guests about games. Otherwise, the process continues after the decision. The process then returns to the initial decision point.

After both parallel paths are completed, the process continues with downloading the games and requesting beer preferences. A decision is then made: if beer is missing, beer is bought; otherwise, the process continues. Finally, the LAN party is prepared, and the process ends.
