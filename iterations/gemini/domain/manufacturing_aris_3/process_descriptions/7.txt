The process starts with preparing materials, followed by assembling a door. After the door is assembled, a decision is made.

If the decision is true, a quality check inquiry is sent and subsequently received. Then, the quality is checked, and a quality report is sent and received. Following the reception of the quality report, the process merges into a single flow.

If the initial decision is false, the door is fixed, and another decision is made, which immediately merges back. This merge point then joins the flow from the quality check process.

After the merge, another decision is made. If this decision is true, another decision is made based on whether the door needs fixing or no fixing is needed.

If the door needs fixing, it is fixed, and then another decision is made. Depending on the outcome of this decision, the process either escalates or proceeds to a merge point. Escalation can occur multiple times before proceeding to the merge point.

If the door does not need fixing, another decision is made, which can also lead to escalation multiple times before proceeding to a merge point.

Both merge points then converge, and the process returns to the decision point regarding whether the door needs fixing or not.

If the initial decision is false, the process ends.
