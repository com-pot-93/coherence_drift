The process starts. First, the product is packaged, then it is shipped. Next, a decision is made. If the product is to be delivered to the door, it is delivered, and another decision is made based on whether a receipt was printed. If the receipt was printed, a pick-up receipt is printed, the receipt is posted, and the product is rerouted to the station. If no receipt was printed, the process proceeds to a merging point. Alternatively, if the initial decision was to deliver to the station, the product is delivered to the station. Another decision is made based on whether the product was received back. If the product was received, it is received, and the process proceeds to a merging point. If the product was not received, the process also proceeds to the same merging point. Finally, the process merges all possible paths and ends.
