The process starts. First, a product is packaged, and then the packaged product is shipped. After the product is shipped, a decision is made: if a receipt is needed, it is printed and then posted. Otherwise, if no receipt is needed, this step is skipped. Regardless of whether a receipt was printed and posted, the next step is to deliver the product to the station. Following the delivery, two parallel paths are initiated.

In the first path, it is determined whether the product was received. If it was, it is processed as received. If not, this step is skipped.

In the second parallel path, it is again determined whether the product was received. If it was, it is processed as received. If not, this step is skipped.

Once both parallel paths have completed, the process ends.
