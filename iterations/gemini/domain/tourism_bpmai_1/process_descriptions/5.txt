The process starts when a customer request is received. The request is then checked. Depending on the outcome of the check, the process either proceeds directly to the next step or requires registration.

If registration is needed, there's a choice between manual and automatic registration. After either manual or automatic registration is completed, the process continues.

Following registration (if it occurred) or directly after the initial check, the process continues to another step. Here, there's a decision to either skip searching for an attraction provider or to proceed with the search.

If the search is performed, the process searches for an attraction provider. After the search, a decision is made based on a condition. If the condition is met, the process continues. If not, an attraction is booked. After booking, the booking status is checked.

Based on the booking status, either a confirmation is sent if the booking is confirmed, or the process continues after a booking rejection if the booking is rejected. After either sending a confirmation or continuing after a rejection, the process continues.

Following the search decision (either skipping the search or after the booking process), the process continues. Finally, a notification is sent, and the process ends.
