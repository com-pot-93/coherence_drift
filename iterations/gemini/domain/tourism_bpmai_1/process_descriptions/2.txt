The process starts when a customer request is received. The request is then checked. Depending on the outcome of the check, the process either proceeds directly to a later stage or goes through a registration step.

If registration is required, a choice is made between performing manual or automatic registration. After either manual or automatic registration is completed, the process continues.

Next, there's an option to search for an attraction provider or skip this search. If the search is performed, the process continues to search for an attraction provider. Following the search, a decision is made based on a condition. If the condition is met, the process proceeds. If not, an attraction is booked. After booking, the booking is either rejected or confirmed. If rejected, the process continues. If confirmed, a confirmation is sent to the customer before proceeding.

After the condition check or after sending the confirmation (or handling a rejection), the process continues. This rejoins the path where the search for an attraction provider was skipped.

Finally, after either proceeding directly from the initial check or after all the registration and attraction booking steps, a notification is sent to the customer, and the process ends.
