The process starts. A call is made to the hospital. Based on the outcome of the call, a decision is made. If the call is accepted, the patient is added to the list and the process continues; otherwise, the process continues directly. Another decision is made, and the process continues regardless of the outcome. If a certain condition is met, a patient control task is performed. Following this, a decision is made. If the decision outcome leads to calling the patient to the hospital, the patient is then hospitalized, followed by childbirth. After childbirth, another decision is made, leading to either providing special treatment or providing post-hospitalization care. After either of these tasks, the process continues. Alternatively, after the patient control task, if the decision outcome is "Yes", the patient control task is repeated, and an escalation event is triggered. After the escalation event, the process continues. The process then loops back to a previous decision point, and continues regardless of the outcome. The process ends.
