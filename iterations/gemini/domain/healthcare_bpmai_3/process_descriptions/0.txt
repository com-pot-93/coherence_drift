The process starts with a call to the hospital, followed by a decision about whether to accept the patient. Depending on the decision, the process either adds the patient to a list or proceeds directly to a merge gateway.

If the patient is added to the list, the process merges with the other branch at a merge gateway. From there, the process continues with patient control, after which a decision is made. If the decision is true, the process loops back to patient control. Otherwise, the patient is called to the hospital and then hospitalized, leading to childbirth.

After childbirth, a decision is made whether the patient needs special treatment or post-hospitalization care. Depending on the decision, the process either provides special treatment or post-hospitalization care. After either of these activities, the process merges at a merge gateway.

Finally, the process merges with the other branch at a merge gateway and ends.
