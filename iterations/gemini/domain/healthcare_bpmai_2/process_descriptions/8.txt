The process starts with conducting a visit, followed by checking the patient's insurance. Then, a decision is made based on whether the patient is registered. If the patient is registered, the process proceeds directly to booking an appointment. If the patient is not registered, another decision is made to check if the patient is already registered. If the patient is not already registered, the patient is registered and then required to pay a fee. If the patient is already registered, the process skips the registration and fee payment. After registering the patient and taking payment, or if the patient was already registered, the process continues to booking an appointment. After booking the appointment, the patient undergoes a doctor's examination. Following the examination, a decision is made regarding whether the patient needs hospitalization. If hospitalization is not required, the patient orders and pays for medicine, then receives the medicine. After receiving the medicine, or if hospitalization is needed, the process continues with booking another appointment, followed by another doctor's examination, and then the process ends.
