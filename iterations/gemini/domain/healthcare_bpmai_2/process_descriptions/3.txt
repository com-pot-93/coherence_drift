The process starts with a nurse visit, followed by checking the patient's insurance. The process then branches based on whether the patient is registered. If the patient is not registered, their registration is checked. Again, the process branches depending on the registration status. If the patient is not registered, they are registered, and a fee is paid. After paying the fee or if the patient was already registered, the process merges back. Similarly, after the initial insurance check, if the patient was registered, the process skips the registration check and merges directly. Following this, an appointment is booked, and the patient undergoes a doctor's examination. The process branches again based on the examination results. The patient may be hospitalized, sent to the pharmacy to order and pay for medicine, and then receive the medicine, or sent home. After hospitalization, receiving medicine, or being sent home, the process merges back. Subsequently, another appointment is booked, followed by another doctor's examination, and the process ends.
