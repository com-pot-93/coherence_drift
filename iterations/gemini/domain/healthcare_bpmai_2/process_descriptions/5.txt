The process starts with a visit to the patient. Following this, the insurance is checked. Subsequently, a decision is made based on whether the patient is registered. If the patient is registered, the process proceeds directly to booking an appointment. However, if the patient is not registered, their registration status is checked. If the patient is not registered, they are registered, and a fee is paid. After paying the fee, the process merges back with the path for registered patients, and an appointment is booked. Following the appointment booking, the patient undergoes a doctor's examination. After the examination, a decision is made. The patient may be hospitalized, sent to the pharmacy to order and pay for medicine, then receive the medicine, or sent home. After any of these options, the process merges, and another appointment is booked, followed by another doctor's examination, and then the process ends.
