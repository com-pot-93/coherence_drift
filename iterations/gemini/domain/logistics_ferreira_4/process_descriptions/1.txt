The process starts with retrieving temperature values. Then, a decision is made. If the condition to generate a standard notification is met, the notification is generated, and the process continues to a merging gateway. Otherwise, the process splits into three parallel paths.

In the first path, the container status is retrieved and then estimated. Based on the estimation, a decision is made. If the condition to generate a container change request is met, the request is generated, and the process continues to a merging gateway. Otherwise, the process proceeds directly to the merging gateway.

In the second parallel path, the client location is retrieved, and the distance is estimated. Based on the estimation, a decision is made. If the condition to estimate the delivery time is met, the delivery time is estimated. Based on the estimation, a decision is made. If the condition to generate a route change request is met, the request is generated, and the process continues to a merging gateway. Otherwise, the process proceeds directly to the merging gateway. If the condition to estimate the delivery time is not met, the process proceeds directly to a merging gateway.

In the third parallel path, the goods status is retrieved and then estimated. Based on the estimation, a decision is made. If the condition to evaluate damages is met, the damages are evaluated. Based on the evaluation, a decision is made. If the condition to generate a removal request is met, the request is generated, and the process continues to a merging gateway. If the condition to generate a removal request is not met, a price change request is generated, and the process continues to a merging gateway. If the condition to evaluate damages is not met, the process proceeds directly to a merging gateway.

After all three parallel paths are completed, the process merges, and the generated messages are sent. Finally, the process continues to a merging gateway, which then leads to the end of the process.
