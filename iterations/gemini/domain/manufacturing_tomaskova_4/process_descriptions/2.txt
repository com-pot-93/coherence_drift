The process starts. It then encounters a decision point. If the "Loop Back" condition is met, it proceeds to another decision point, which leads to another decision point. If "true" is the outcome, the process continues with a series of tasks: "Production Planning", "Assign Workers", "Production Design", "Product Delivery", "Production Logout", and "Quality Control". After "Quality Control", there's another decision point. If the "Loop Back" condition is met, the process returns to the "Production Planning" task, and an escalation event occurs before reaching another decision point. This decision point then loops back to the second decision point. If the "true" condition is not met, the process continues to another decision point.

Alternatively, from the initial decision point, the process can split into two parallel paths. One path involves the "Register to Finished Stock" task followed by the "Stock to Finished Goods" task. The other path includes the "Register to Semi-Finished Stock" task followed by the "Load to Semi-Finished Stock" task. After both paths are completed, they merge back together.

Finally, the process reaches a decision point before it ends.
