The process starts. It then encounters a decision point. If the condition to loop back is met, it proceeds to another decision point, which then merges into another decision point. If the condition at the second decision point is true, it proceeds to a third decision point. If the condition at the third decision point is true, a production planning task is performed, followed by another decision point. From there, the process moves to an assign workers task, then to a production design task, a product delivery task, a production logout task, and finally a quality control task. After quality control, there is another decision point. If the condition to loop back is met, the process returns to the production planning task. Alternatively, an escalation event can occur, which then merges back into the process flow. If the condition at the decision point after the production planning task is not met, the process merges back into the flow. If the condition at the third decision point is false, the process merges back into the flow. If the condition at the second decision point is not true, the process merges back into the flow. If the initial condition to loop back is not met, the process merges into the flow. After the initial decision point, the process splits into two parallel paths. One path involves registering to finished stock, stocking to finished goods, and then joining back with the other path. The other path involves registering to semi-finished stock, loading to semi-finished stock, and then joining back with the first path. Finally, the process ends.
