The process starts. First, the system searches for a customer, then makes an offer to the customer. Depending on the outcome, the process either proceeds directly to a gateway or the customer makes an additional request, which is followed by providing the details. After providing details, the process continues to another gateway. From there, the process either proceeds to another gateway or the customer makes a booking request. If a booking request is made, the bill is sent to the customer. Depending on the outcome, the process either proceeds to another gateway or the customer makes a payment. If the payment is made, depending on the outcome, the process either contacts the customer or books the travel and sends a booking confirmation. After contacting the customer or sending a booking confirmation, the process proceeds to a gateway, which then loops back to the gateway before the payment. The gateway before the payment loops back to the gateway before the booking request. The gateway before the booking request proceeds to the gateway after the initial offer. The gateway after the initial offer then ends the process.
