The process starts. Then, it splits into two parallel activities.
In the first branch, a physical examination is performed. After the examination is complete, this branch merges back with the other branch.
The second branch proceeds directly to the merge point.
Once both branches are complete, a diagnosis is made. Based on the diagnosis, the process follows one of two exclusive paths.
If the diagnosis is A, a surgical intervention is performed.
If the diagnosis is B, surgery is planned.
After either the surgical intervention or the surgery planning is complete, the process merges. Finally, the patient is discharged, and the process ends.
