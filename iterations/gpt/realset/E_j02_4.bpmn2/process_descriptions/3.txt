The process begins. Two activities are carried out at the same time: one is to check if the baby is younger than two years, and the other is to identify the parent. Once both of these activities are completed, the process continues.

Next, a decision is made based on the parent's situation. If the parent is self-employed, studying, a housewife, or a man, the process moves directly to the end. Otherwise, the process continues with the following steps:

A notification is sent to the employer. After that, the process waits for confirmation from the employer. Once confirmation is received, the parent takes maternity leave.

After the parent takes maternity leave, another decision is made based on how the leave is split:

- If the split is less than two months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the split. Once the notification is received, the parent takes leave after the split of less than two months.
- If the split is more than three months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the split. Once the notification is received, the parent takes leave after the split of more than three months.
- If the split does not fall into these categories, the process continues to another decision point.

At this next decision point, the process considers whether there is an extension to the leave:

- If the extension is less than two months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the extension. Once the notification is received, the parent takes leave after the extension of less than two months.
- If the extension is more than three months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the extension. Once the notification is received, the parent takes leave after the extension of more than three months.
- If the extension does not fall into these categories, the process continues to another decision point.

At this final decision point, the process checks if both parents are taking leave together:

- If both parents take leave together, they do so at this stage.
- If the situation is different, the process continues without any specific action.

After all these steps and decisions, the process comes to an end.
