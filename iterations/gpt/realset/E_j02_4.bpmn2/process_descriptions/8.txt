The process begins. Immediately, two activities are carried out at the same time: one is to check if the baby is younger than two years, and the other is to identify the parent. Once both of these activities are completed, the process continues.

Next, a decision is made based on the results of the previous steps. If the necessary conditions are met, the process proceeds by sending a notification to the employer. After the notification is sent, the process waits for confirmation from the employer. Once confirmation is received, the parent takes maternity leave.

Following this, another decision is made regarding the specifics of the leave. There are several possible scenarios:

- If the leave is split and the split is less than two months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the split of less than two months. After receiving the notification, the parent takes leave following the split of less than two months.
- If the leave is split and the split is more than three months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the split of more than three months. After receiving the notification, the parent takes leave following the split of more than three months.
- If the leave is extended and the extension is less than two months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the extension of less than two months. After receiving the notification, the parent takes leave following the extension of less than two months.
- If the leave is extended and the extension is more than three months, the employer is notified about this. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding the extension of more than three months. After receiving the notification, the parent takes leave following the extension of more than three months.
- If both parents take leave together, this is recorded and the process continues.
- If none of these situations apply, the process continues without any additional action.

After any of these scenarios, the process proceeds regardless of the outcome, and then moves to the next stage.

If, at the earlier decision point, the conditions are not met, the process continues with the next steps, proceeds regardless of the outcome, and then moves to the next stage.

Finally, the process ends.
