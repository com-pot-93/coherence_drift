The process begins. Two activities are carried out at the same time: one is to check if the baby is younger than two years, and the other is to identify the parent. Once both of these activities are completed, the process continues.

Next, a decision is made based on the parent's situation. If the parent is self-employed, studying, a housewife, or a man, the process moves directly to the end. Otherwise, the process continues with the following steps:

A notification is sent to the employer. After that, the process waits for confirmation from the employer. Once the confirmation is received, the parent takes maternity leave.

After the parent takes maternity leave, another decision is made. There are three possible paths:

1. If the leave is split and the split is less than two months, the employer is notified about the split being less than two months. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding this split. Once the notification is received, the parent takes maternity leave after the split of less than two months.

2. If the leave is split and the split is more than three months, the employer is notified about the split being more than three months. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding this split. Once the notification is received, the parent takes maternity leave after the split of more than three months.

3. If neither of the above conditions applies, the process continues to the next decision.

At the next decision point, there are three possible paths:

1. If the leave is extended and the extension is less than two months, the employer is notified about the extension being less than two months. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding this extension. Once the notification is received, the parent takes maternity leave after the extension of less than two months.

2. If the leave is extended and the extension is more than three months, the employer is notified about the extension being more than three months. The process then waits for a notification from the employer regarding this extension. Once the notification is received, the parent takes maternity leave after the extension of more than three months.

3. If neither of the above conditions applies, the process continues.

Alternatively, if both parents are taking leave together, both parents take maternity leave at the same time.

After any of these paths are completed, the process moves to the end and finishes.
