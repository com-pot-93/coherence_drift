The process begins with a starting point. Immediately, two activities are prepared in parallel:

1. The first activity is to check if the baby is younger than two years.
2. At the same time, the parent is identified.

Once both of these activities are completed, the process continues.

Next, a decision is made based on the parent's situation. If the parent is self-employed, studying, a housewife, or a man, the process skips ahead to the end. Otherwise, the process continues with the following steps:

- A notification is sent to the employer.
- The process waits for confirmation from the employer.
- After receiving confirmation, the parent takes maternity leave.

After the parent has taken maternity leave, another decision is made. There are three possible paths:

1. If the leave is to be split, another decision is made based on the length of the split:
   - If the split is less than two months, a notification is sent to the employer for a split leave of less than two months. The process waits to receive a notification from the employer for this split leave, and then the parent takes maternity leave again after the split of less than two months.
   - If the split is more than three months, a notification is sent to the employer for a split leave of more than three months. The process waits to receive a notification from the employer for this split leave, and then the parent takes maternity leave again after the split of more than three months.

2. If the leave is to be extended, another decision is made based on the length of the extension:
   - If the extension is less than two months, a notification is sent to the employer for an extension of less than two months. The process waits to receive a notification from the employer for this extension, and then the parent takes maternity leave again after the extension of less than two months.
   - If the extension is more than three months, a notification is sent to the employer for an extension of more than three months. The process waits to receive a notification from the employer for this extension, and then the parent takes maternity leave again after the extension of more than three months.

3. If both parents are taking leave at the same time, both parents take maternity leave together.

After any of these paths are completed, the process continues to the next step.

Finally, the process reaches its end point.
