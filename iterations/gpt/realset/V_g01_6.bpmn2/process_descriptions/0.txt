The process begins when a customer arrives. The first step is to ask the customer whether they want only a burger or a full menu.

If the customer chooses a menu, two things happen at the same time. One person prepares a drink for the customer, while another asks the customer whether they would like wedges or fries. If the customer chooses fries, the fries are prepared; if the customer chooses wedges, the wedges are prepared. After the chosen side is prepared, the process continues.

Once both the drink and the chosen side (fries or wedges) are ready, the process moves forward.

If the customer chose only a burger at the beginning, the process skips the preparation of drinks and sides and moves directly to the next step.

Next, the burger is prepared. At the same time, there is a decision about whether to provide enthusiastic status updates to the customer. If the decision is to give updates, the updates are provided, and this can happen repeatedly, possibly escalating if needed, before moving on. If no updates are given, or after updates are completed, the process continues.

Once the burger is ready and any status updates are handled, the order is placed on the conveyor belt for delivery to the customer. The process ends at this point.
