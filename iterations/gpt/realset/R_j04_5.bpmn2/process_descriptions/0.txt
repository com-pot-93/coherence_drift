The process begins. First, an initial action is performed. After this, there is a step to check if the car is still registered.

Based on the outcome of this check, the process can follow two different paths:

- If the process continues (the specific condition is not detailed), the car owner is notified. After notifying the car owner, there is a check to see if the car has been brought to a mechanic.

  - If the car has not been brought to a mechanic, the process continues with another action, followed by issuing a fine to the car owner. After the fine is issued, the process moves forward.

  - If the car has been brought to a mechanic, information about the car’s problems is received. Then, a payment offer is made to the car owner. At this point, there are two possible paths:

    - In one path, payment is transferred to the mechanic, and the process continues.
    - In the other path, the process continues without transferring payment.

    After either of these, confirmation of a successful repair is received. Then, the time for the next service is received. The process then moves forward.

  After either the fine is issued or the next service time is received, the process continues to the next step.

- If the car is not still registered (or the alternative condition applies), the process skips the notification and mechanic steps and moves directly to the next step.

Finally, the process ends.
