The process begins with an initial action being performed. After this, a decision is made regarding the registration status of the car.

If the car is not registered, the process moves directly to a later stage, bypassing several steps.

If the car is registered, the car owner is notified. Following this notification, another decision is made about whether the car has been brought to a mechanic.

If the car has not been brought to a mechanic, an alternative action is taken, and then a fine is issued. After the fine is issued, the process continues to the next stage.

If the car has been brought to a mechanic, information about the car’s problem is received. After this, a payment offer is made. Then, a decision is made about whether the payment has been transferred.

If the payment has been transferred, the payment is sent to the mechanic, and the process continues.

If the payment has not been transferred, the process continues without sending the payment.

Regardless of the path taken—whether a fine was issued, payment was sent, or payment was not sent—the process converges at a point where repair confirmation is received. After receiving the repair confirmation, information about the next service is provided. Then, the next step is carried out, and the process ends.
