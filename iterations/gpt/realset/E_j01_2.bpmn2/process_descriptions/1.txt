The process begins when a job application is received. Once the application is received, two activities take place at the same time: the application is confirmed, and the application is rated. Both of these actions are carried out independently but must both be completed before the process continues.

After both the confirmation and the rating of the application are finished, the process reaches a decision point. At this stage, there are two possible paths:

- If the process is to end here, the process moves directly to the end.
- If the process is to continue, the next step is to negotiate an interview with the job seeker.

If the process continues, an interview is negotiated with the job seeker. After the negotiation, the job seeker is rated. Once the rating is complete, another decision is made:

- If the decision is to hire the job seeker, the next step is to hire the prospective customer.
- If the decision is not to hire, the process skips the hiring step.

Regardless of whether the job seeker is hired or not, the process then moves to the final step and ends.
