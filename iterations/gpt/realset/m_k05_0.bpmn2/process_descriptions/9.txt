The process begins with the collection of requirements. Once the requirements have been gathered, a decision is made about whether to continue. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends immediately.

If the decision is to proceed, the requirements are sent forward. After this, a draft is received. Another decision is then made about whether to continue. If the decision is not to continue at this point, the process ends.

If the process continues, another decision is made based on a specific condition. If this condition is not met, the process ends.

If the condition is met, the draft is evaluated. After the evaluation, a decision is made about whether to proceed. If the decision is not to proceed, the process ends.

If the decision is to proceed, the process moves through three checkpoints in sequence: Checkpoint 1, Checkpoint 2, and Checkpoint 3. After completing Checkpoint 3, the process ends.
