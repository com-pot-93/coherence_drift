The process begins with the start of the planning. First, invitations are sent out to friends. After the invitations have been sent, the next step is to ask friends for suggestions on which games to play at the event. Once these suggestions have been collected, the organizer receives the game suggestions from the friends.

With all the suggestions in hand, the organizer then chooses which games will be played at the party. At this point, there is a decision to make: if any of the chosen games require downloads, the necessary games are downloaded. If no downloads are needed, this step is skipped. In either case, the process continues to the next stage.

Next, the organizer asks friends about their needs for beer. After gathering this information, the organizer estimates the total amount of beer that will be needed for the party. Another decision is made here: if beer needs to be bought, the organizer goes to the supermarket to purchase it. If there is already enough beer, this step is skipped. Regardless of the outcome, the process moves forward.

Finally, the organizer completes the final preparations for the LAN party. Once all preparations are finished, the process comes to an end.
