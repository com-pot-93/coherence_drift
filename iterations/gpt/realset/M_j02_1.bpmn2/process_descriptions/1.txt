The process begins when a customized airplane order is received. After receiving the order, the order is processed. Once the order has been processed, two activities happen at the same time: the base model of the airplane is built, and the customization requirements are transmitted.

Both of these activities must be completed before the process continues. Once both the base model is built and the customization requirements have been transmitted, the process checks whether a certain condition is met. If the condition is true, the process waits for 14 days. After waiting, another check is performed. If the required outcome has not been achieved, a reminder is sent to the responsible team, followed by another 14-day waiting period. If the situation is still unresolved, the process escalates the issue and loops back to the previous check, repeating the reminder and waiting steps as necessary until the condition is met.

Once the condition is satisfied, the process continues with the installation of the customized parts. After the customized parts are installed, the engines and risk factors are checked. The process then prepares for a test flight. Once preparations are complete, the test flight protocol is received and then forwarded to the customer.

Next, the process determines the shipping method. If standard shipping is chosen, the airplane is shipped via standard shipping. If premium shipping is selected, the airplane is shipped via premium shipping. Regardless of the shipping method, the process then waits to receive an email confirmation. Once the email confirmation is received, the process ends.
