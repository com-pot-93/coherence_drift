The process begins when the assessment tool is initiated. After starting, the next step is to specify the type of incident that has occurred.

At this point, a decision is made regarding the incident type. There are four possible options:

1. If the incident is classified as an "Accident," the process continues with confirming that an accident has occurred.
2. If the incident is classified as an "Almost Accident," the process continues with confirming that an almost accident has occurred.
3. If the incident is classified as a "Severe Safety or Health Risk," the process continues with confirming that a severe safety or health risk has occurred.
4. If the incident is classified as "Defective Equipment," the process continues with confirming that defective equipment has been identified.

Regardless of which type is selected and confirmed, the process then moves forward to providing details about the incident.

After the incident details are provided, another decision is made about how to proceed:

- If the choice is to "Proceed Directly," the next step is to review a list of tasks.
- If the choice is to "Contact Private Insurance First," the next step is to contact private insurance.

After either reviewing the list of tasks or contacting private insurance, the process continues to executing the proposed tasks.

Once the proposed tasks have been executed, the process comes to an end.
