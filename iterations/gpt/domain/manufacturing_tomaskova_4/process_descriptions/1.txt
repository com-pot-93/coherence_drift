The process begins with a starting point. Immediately after the start, a decision is made. Following this, another decision is taken.

If the outcome of this second decision is positive (true), the process continues to a third decision point. At this third decision, there are two possible paths:

- If a certain condition is met, the process moves to a fourth decision point.
- If the condition is not met, the process proceeds through a series of steps:
  1. Production planning is carried out.
  2. Workers are assigned.
  3. A production design is created.
  4. The product is delivered.
  5. Production logout is performed.
  6. Quality control is conducted.
  
  After quality control, the process returns to the fourth decision point.

At the fourth decision point, the process loops back to the first decision point, creating a cycle.

Returning to the second decision, if the outcome is not positive, the process moves to a fifth decision point. At this point, there are two possible outcomes:

- If the product is finished, it is registered in the finished stock, then moved to the finished goods stock, and the process continues to the next step.
- If the product is semi-finished, it is registered in the semi-finished stock, then loaded into the semi-finished stock, and the process continues to the next step.

After either registering and moving the finished product or registering and loading the semi-finished product, the process reaches its end point.
