The process begins with a starting point. Immediately after, a decision is made to determine how the process should proceed.

From this decision, the process moves to another decision point. If the outcome is positive ("true"), the process continues to a third decision. This third decision leads to two possible paths:

1. **First Path:**  
   The process moves to a fourth decision. This decision has three possible outcomes:
   
   - **If a certain condition is not met:**  
     The process proceeds to production planning. After planning, workers are assigned, followed by the creation of a production design. The product is then delivered, and the production is logged out. A quality control check is performed, after which the process continues to the next stage.
   
   - **If an issue needs to be escalated:**  
     The process moves directly to escalating the issue, and then continues to the next stage.
   
   - **Otherwise:**  
     The process continues to a fifth decision.

2. **Second Path:**  
   The process moves directly to the fifth decision.

At the fifth decision, there are two possible outcomes:

- **If the product is finished:**  
  The finished product is registered, then moved to the finished goods stock. Another decision is made:
  
  - **If the product is still considered finished:**  
    The issue is escalated, and the process continues through a series of steps before returning to the previous stage.
  
  - **If the product is not finished:**  
    The process returns to the previous stage.

- **If the product is semi-finished:**  
  The semi-finished product is registered, then loaded into the semi-finished stock. Another decision is made:
  
  - **If the product is now finished:**  
    The issue is escalated, and the process continues through a series of steps before returning to the previous stage.
  
  - **If the product is not finished:**  
    The process returns to the previous stage.

After these steps, the process continues back through the earlier decisions, eventually reaching the second decision point.

If, at the third decision, the outcome is negative ("false"), the process loops back to the first decision point.

Finally, after all these steps and decisions, the process reaches its end point.
