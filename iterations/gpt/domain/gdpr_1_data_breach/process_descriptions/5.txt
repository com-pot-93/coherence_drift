The process begins when an incident is detected. After detecting the incident, the next step is to retrieve the data that has been breached. Once the breached data is retrieved, the process continues by identifying the individuals who have been affected by the incident.

After identifying the affected individuals, two activities are carried out at the same time. One activity is to take action to limit any further loss of data. The other activity is to notify the national authority about the incident. Both of these activities are performed independently and in parallel.

Once both actions are completed, the process continues by evaluating the data that was stolen. Based on this evaluation, a decision is made about whether a notification is required. If a notification is required, a notification is sent regarding the evaluation of the stolen data. If no notification is needed, the process skips this step. Regardless of the decision, the process then moves forward.

Next, the process involves evaluating the security standards. Again, a decision is made about whether a notification is required based on this evaluation. If a notification is needed, it is sent regarding the evaluation of the security standards. If not, the process continues without sending a notification.

After this, the process proceeds to request clarification. Once clarification is requested, a response to the clarification is received. Based on the response, another decision is made about whether a notification is required. If a notification is needed, it is sent regarding the clarification. If not, the process continues without sending a notification.

Finally, after all these steps and decisions, the process comes to an end.
