The process begins when an incident is detected. After detecting the incident, the next step is to retrieve the data that has been breached. Once the breached data is retrieved, the process continues by identifying the individuals who have been affected.

After identifying the affected individuals, two activities are carried out at the same time. One activity is to limit any further loss of data, and the other is to notify the national authority about the incident. Both of these activities must be completed before the process continues.

Once both activities are finished, the next step is to evaluate the data that was stolen. Based on this evaluation, a decision is made about whether a notification regarding the stolen data is required. If a notification is required, a notification about the stolen data is sent. If no notification is required, the process continues without sending one. In either case, the process moves forward to the next step.

The next step is to evaluate the security standards. Again, a decision is made about whether a notification regarding security standards is required. If a notification is required, it is sent. If not, the process continues without sending one. Regardless of the decision, the process proceeds to the next step.

After this, a clarification is requested. Once the clarification response is received, another decision is made about whether a notification is required based on the clarification. If a notification is required, it is sent. If not, the process continues without sending one. In either case, the process then comes to an end.
