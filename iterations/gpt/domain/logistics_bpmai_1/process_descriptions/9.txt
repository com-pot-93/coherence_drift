The process begins when a shipment request is received. After receiving the request, the next step is to check if the requested product is available.

If the product is not available, the process moves directly towards the end.

If the product is available, the shipment address is verified. Once the address is verified, the type of shipment is determined.

If the shipment type cannot be determined, the process involves confirming the shipment to a service station. After this confirmation, the process continues as if the shipment type had been determined.

Once the shipment type is determined (either directly or after confirmation), the inbound shipment is assembled. After assembling, the shipment is sent out.

Following the dispatch, the process checks the inbound shipment. If the check fails, the process repeats the inbound shipment check. If the check passes, the process continues.

Next, the products are prepared for the service station. If more preparation is needed, this step is repeated until preparation is complete.

After preparing the products for the service station, the process may require regional shipment preparation. If regional preparation is not complete, this step is repeated until it is finished.

Once all preparations are complete, the process moves towards its conclusion. The process then ends.
