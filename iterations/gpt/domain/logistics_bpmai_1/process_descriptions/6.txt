The process begins when a shipment request is received. After receiving the request, the next step is to check if the requested product is available.

If the product is not available, the process moves directly towards completion.

If the product is available, a further check is performed. This further check can lead to two outcomes:  
- If the result is negative, the further check is repeated.  
- If the result is positive, the process continues to verify the shipment address.

Once the shipment address is verified, the shipment type is determined. If the type cannot be determined, the shipment is confirmed to be sent to a service station. If the type is determined successfully, the process continues.

After this, the inbound shipment is assembled and then sent. Once the shipment is sent, a check on the inbound shipment is performed. If this check fails, it is repeated until it passes.

When the inbound shipment check is successful, the products are prepared for the service station. If more preparation is needed, this step is repeated until no further preparation is required.

At this point, it is checked whether regional preparation is needed. If so, regional shipment preparation is carried out, followed by a check of the regional shipment preparation. Once this is complete, or if no regional preparation is needed, the process continues.

Finally, the process reaches its end after all necessary checks and preparations have been completed.
