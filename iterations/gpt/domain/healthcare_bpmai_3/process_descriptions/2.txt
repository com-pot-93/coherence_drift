The process begins when a call is received. After the call is received, a decision is made about whether the patient is accepted or not.

- If the patient is not accepted, the process moves directly towards completion.
- If the patient is accepted, the patient is added to a list.

After adding the patient to the list, another decision is made. If the outcome is positive, checks are performed on the patient.

Once the checks are performed, a series of decisions follow, each evaluating the results of the checks:

- If the checks are positive, the process continues to another decision point.
    - If the outcome remains positive, another decision is made about whether to proceed to the hospital.
        - If the decision is to proceed, the patient is called to the hospital.
        - The patient is then hospitalized.
        - After hospitalization, childbirth takes place.
        - Following childbirth, there is a decision about the next step:
            - If post-hospitalization care is needed, this care is provided.
            - If special treatment is required, special treatment is provided.
        - After either post-hospitalization care or special treatment, another decision is made:
            - If further checks are needed, the process returns to performing checks.
            - If the process is complete, a series of steps are followed to finalize the process, eventually leading to the end of the process.

- If at any point the checks are negative or the decision is made not to proceed to the hospital, the case is escalated.
    - After escalation, checks are performed again.
    - The process then continues through the same series of decisions as before, potentially looping back to earlier steps if necessary.

Throughout the process, if a decision leads to the process being complete or the patient not being accepted, the process moves towards its conclusion and ends.

In summary, the process involves receiving a call, deciding on patient acceptance, adding the patient to a list if accepted, performing checks, making decisions based on the results, possibly escalating the case and repeating checks, and, if appropriate, proceeding through hospitalization, childbirth, and post-care steps, with the process ending when all necessary actions are complete.
