The process begins when a call is made to the hospital. After the call, a decision is made about whether the patient will be accepted. Based on this decision, the process can follow one of two paths:

If the patient is accepted, the patient is added to a list of patients. After being added to the list, there is a repeated cycle where the patient's condition is checked. This cycle continues until a certain condition is met, at which point the patient is called to the hospital.

Once the patient is called to the hospital, the patient is hospitalized. After hospitalization, childbirth takes place. Following childbirth, there is a choice between two possible actions: either the patient receives post-hospitalization care, or the patient receives special treatment. Regardless of which action is taken, the process continues to the next step.

If the patient is not accepted at the initial decision point, the process moves directly to the end.

After either post-hospitalization care or special treatment, the process concludes.
