The process begins when a customer request is received. After receiving the request, the details of the request are checked.

At this point, a decision is made about whether to continue with the process. If the decision is not to continue, the process moves directly towards completion. If the process is to continue, another decision is made to determine how the registration should be handled.

If manual registration is chosen, the registration is completed manually. If automatic registration is chosen, the registration is completed automatically. After either type of registration, the process continues to the next step.

Next, a decision is made about whether to search for an attraction provider. If the decision is not to search, a notification is sent to the customer, and the process moves towards completion. If the decision is to search, the process continues with searching for an attraction provider.

After searching for an attraction provider, another decision is made about whether to attempt a booking. If the decision is not to book, the process moves towards completion. If the decision is to attempt a booking, the booking is attempted.

Once the booking attempt is made, a decision is made based on the outcome. If the booking is rejected, the rejection is received, and the process moves towards completion. If the booking is confirmed, the confirmation is received, and a confirmation is sent to the customer. After sending the confirmation, the process moves towards completion.

The process ends after all these steps are completed, depending on the decisions made at each point.
