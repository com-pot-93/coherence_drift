The process begins when a customer request is received. After receiving the request, the details of the request are checked.

At this point, a decision is made about whether to continue with the process. If the decision is not to continue, the process moves directly towards completion.

If the process continues, another decision is made to determine how the registration should be handled. If the registration is to be done manually, the manual registration is performed. If the registration is to be done automatically, the automatic registration is performed. After either type of registration, the process continues to the next step.

Next, another decision is made about whether to search for an attraction provider. If the decision is not to search, a notification is sent to the customer, and the process moves towards completion.

If the decision is to search for an attraction provider, the search is carried out. After searching, a decision is made about whether to book the attraction. If the decision is not to book, the process moves towards completion.

If the decision is to book the attraction, the booking is made. After attempting to book, a decision is made based on the outcome of the booking. If the booking is rejected, the rejection is received, and the process moves towards completion.

If the booking is confirmed, the confirmation is received, and a confirmation is sent to the customer. After this, the process moves towards completion.

The process ends after all these steps are completed, depending on the decisions made at each point.
