The process begins when a travel request is received. After receiving the request, a tour plan is prepared. Once the tour plan is ready, it is sent out.

At this point, a decision is made about whether to continue with the process. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends immediately. If the process continues, another decision is made to check for a negative outcome. If there is a negative outcome, the process ends. If not, the process waits for a response.

After waiting for a response, another decision is made about whether to continue. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends. If the process continues, another check is made to decide whether to continue. If not, the process ends. If the process continues, the tour is booked.

Once the tour is booked, another decision is made about whether to continue. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends. If the process continues, the customer is charged.

After charging the customer, another decision is made about whether to continue. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends. If the process continues, there is a check to determine if the customer should be contacted. If the customer needs to be contacted, this is done, and then the process continues. If no further action is needed, the process continues directly.

Next, two actions happen at the same time: a bill is sent, and a booking confirmation is sent. After both actions are completed, there is another decision about whether to contact the customer. If the customer needs to be contacted, this is done, and then the process continues. If no further action is needed, the process continues directly.

Again, two actions happen at the same time: a bill is sent, and a booking confirmation is sent. After both actions are completed, there is another decision about whether to contact the customer. If the customer needs to be contacted, this is done, and then the process continues. If no further action is needed, the process continues directly.

Once more, two actions happen at the same time: a bill is sent, and a booking confirmation is sent. After both actions are completed, there is another decision about whether to contact the customer. If the customer needs to be contacted, this is done, and then the process continues. If no further action is needed, the process continues directly.

Finally, two actions happen at the same time: a bill is sent, and a booking confirmation is sent. After both actions are completed, the process ends.
