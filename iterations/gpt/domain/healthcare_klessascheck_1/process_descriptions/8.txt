The process begins with the identification of non-specific low back pain. After this, there is a decision point where one of two approaches is chosen.

In the first approach, superficial heat or another non-drug treatment is applied. Following this, there is another decision about whether medication is needed. If medication is needed, a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) or a skeletal muscle relaxant (SMR) is prescribed. If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed. Regardless of the choice, the process continues to the next step.

In the second approach, exercise or another non-drug treatment is applied. After this, there is a similar decision about the need for medication. If medication is needed, an NSAID is prescribed. If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed. Again, regardless of the choice, the process continues to the next step.

After either approach and the related decisions, the process proceeds to the next stage. Finally, the process ends.
