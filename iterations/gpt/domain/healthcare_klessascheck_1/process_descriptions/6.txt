The process begins when a case of non-specific low back pain is identified. After this, there is a decision point where it is determined which type of initial non-drug treatment should be applied. There are two possible paths:

1. In the first path, superficial heat or another non-drug treatment is applied. After this treatment, there is another decision point to assess whether medication is needed.  
   - If medication is needed, a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) or a skeletal muscle relaxant (SMR) is prescribed.  
   - If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed.  
   After either of these actions, the process continues to the next step.

2. In the second path, exercise or another non-drug treatment is applied. After this treatment, there is a similar decision point to determine if medication is required.  
   - If medication is needed, an NSAID is prescribed.  
   - If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed.  
   After either of these actions, the process continues to the next step.

Once either of the two main paths is completed, the process moves forward to the next stage. The process ends after this step.
