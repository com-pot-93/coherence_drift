The process begins when a case of non-specific low back pain is identified.

After this, there is a decision point where it is determined which type of non-drug treatment to apply. There are two possible paths:

1. **First Path:**  
   - Superficial heat or another non-drug treatment is applied.
   - After this treatment, there is another decision point to assess whether medication is needed.
     - If medication is needed, a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) or a skeletal muscle relaxant (SMR) is prescribed.
     - If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed.
   - Regardless of whether medication is prescribed or not, the process continues to the next main decision point.

2. **Second Path:**  
   - Exercise or another non-drug treatment is applied.
   - After this treatment, there is a decision point to determine if medication is needed.
     - If medication is needed, an NSAID is prescribed.
     - If medication is not needed, no medication is prescribed.
   - Regardless of whether medication is prescribed or not, the process continues to the next main decision point.

After both paths converge at the main decision point, the process proceeds to the next stage.

Finally, the process ends.
