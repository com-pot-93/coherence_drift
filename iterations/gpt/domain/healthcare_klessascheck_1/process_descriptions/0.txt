The process begins when a person is diagnosed with non-specific low back pain. After this diagnosis, there is a decision point where the next step is chosen.

From here, one possible path is to apply superficial heat or other non-drug treatments. After this, another decision is made. If needed, the next step is to prescribe either a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) or a skeletal muscle relaxant (SMR). After this prescription, there is another decision point, after which the process continues to the next stage.

Alternatively, after applying superficial heat or other non-drug treatments, it may be decided to move directly to the next stage without prescribing medication.

Returning to the first decision after diagnosis, another possible path is to apply exercise or other non-drug treatments. After this, another decision is made. If necessary, the next step is to prescribe an NSAID. Following this, there is another decision point. If needed, an opioid may be prescribed. After this prescription, there is another decision, after which the process continues to the next stage.

Alternatively, after prescribing an NSAID, it may be decided to move directly to the next stage without prescribing an opioid.

Similarly, after applying exercise or other non-drug treatments, it may be decided to move directly to the next stage without prescribing any medication.

After all these possible paths, the process comes together at a common point and then ends.
