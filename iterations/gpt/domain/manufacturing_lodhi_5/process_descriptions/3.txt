The process begins when a production order is received. After receiving the production order, the next step is to prepare the production order.

Once the production order is prepared, a decision is made. If the process is to continue, the next step is to collect raw materials. If the process is not to continue, it loops back to the previous decision point, allowing for another evaluation.

If the process continues, after collecting raw materials, the materials are cut. Once the materials are cut, two activities happen at the same time: stitching and printing. Both stitching and printing are carried out independently and simultaneously.

After both stitching and printing are completed, the process moves forward to complete the production. Once production is complete, a quality check is performed. Following the quality check, a quality report is created.

After the quality report is created, another decision is made. If adjustments are needed, the settings are adjusted, and then the process moves to the next decision point. If no adjustments are needed, the data is sent to the warehouse, and then the process also moves to the next decision point.

At this decision point, the process checks again whether to continue or not. If the process is not to continue, it loops back to the earlier decision point for another evaluation. If the process is to continue, the process ends.
