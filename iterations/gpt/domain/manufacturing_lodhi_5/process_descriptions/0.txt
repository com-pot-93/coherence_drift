The process begins when a production order is received. After receiving the production order, the next step is to prepare the production order for processing.

Once the production order is prepared, a decision is made. If the decision leads to continuing the process, raw materials are collected. After collecting the raw materials, the materials are cut as required.

Following the cutting step, two activities take place at the same time: stitching and printing. Both stitching and printing are performed independently and simultaneously. Once both stitching and printing are completed, the process continues.

The next step is to complete the production. After production is completed, the quality of the finished product is checked. Once the quality check is done, a quality report is created.

After the quality report is created, another decision is made. Based on this decision, one of two actions is taken:

- If adjustments are needed, the settings are adjusted. After adjusting the settings, the process returns to the previous decision point for further evaluation.
- If no adjustments are needed, the data is sent to the warehouse. After sending the data to the warehouse, the process also returns to the previous decision point for further evaluation.

At the earlier decision point, if the process does not need to continue, it ends.

This cycle allows for repeated quality checks and adjustments until the process is ready to be completed and ended.
