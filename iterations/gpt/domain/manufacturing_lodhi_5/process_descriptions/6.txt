The process begins when a production order is received. After receiving the order, the next step is to prepare the production order.

Once the production order is prepared, a decision is made based on a certain condition. If the condition is met, the process continues with collecting raw materials. After collecting the raw materials, the materials are cut.

Following the cutting of materials, two activities are carried out at the same time: stitching and printing. Both stitching and printing proceed independently and in parallel. Once both stitching and printing are completed, the process continues.

Next, another decision point is reached. If the outcome is positive, the process moves forward to complete the production. After production is completed, a quality check is performed. Once the quality check is done, a quality report is created.

After the quality report is created, another decision is made. The process continues to the next decision point, where it is determined whether any adjustments are needed. If adjustments are needed, the next step is to determine what the next step should be.

At this point, another decision is made. If it is decided to adjust settings, the settings are adjusted. If no further action is needed, the process skips the adjustment step. In either case, the process continues with sending data to the warehouse.

After sending data to the warehouse, another decision is made. If further actions are required, the process returns to the step of completing production, and the cycle continues from there. If no further actions are required, the process moves to another decision point, which leads to the end of the process.

If, at any of the decision points, the condition is not met, the process moves directly to the end. Additionally, there is a loop in the process: after certain escalations, the process can return to earlier decision points, allowing for repeated checks and actions as necessary before finally ending.
