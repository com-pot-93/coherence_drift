The process begins when a production order is received. After receiving the production order, the next step is to prepare the production order.

Once the preparation is complete, a decision is made about whether to continue with the process. If the decision is not to continue, the process ends here. If the decision is to continue, another decision is made to confirm whether to continue again. If the decision at this point is not to continue, the process loops back to the previous decision about whether to continue.

If the decision is to proceed, the next step is to collect the raw materials needed for production. After collecting the raw materials, the materials are cut as required for the production process.

Following the cutting of materials, two activities take place at the same time: stitching and printing. Both stitching and printing are performed independently and simultaneously. Once both stitching and printing are completed, the process continues.

The next step is to complete the production. After production is completed, a quality check is performed. Once the quality check is done, a quality report is created.

After the quality report is created, it is determined whether any adjustments are needed. If adjustments are needed, the settings are adjusted, and the process returns to the previous decision about whether to continue again. If no adjustments are needed, the data is sent to the warehouse, and the process returns to the previous decision about whether to continue again.

This cycle of decisions and actions continues until the decision is made not to continue, at which point the process ends.
