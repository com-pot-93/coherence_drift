The process begins when a production order is received. After receiving the production order, the next step is to prepare the production order for processing.

Once the production order is prepared, a decision is made. If the condition is met (the process continues only when the condition is true), the process moves forward. If not, it loops back and waits until the condition is satisfied.

After the condition is met, raw materials are collected. Once the raw materials are collected, the materials are cut to the required specifications.

Following the cutting of materials, two activities take place at the same time: stitching and printing. Both stitching and printing are performed independently and simultaneously.

When both stitching and printing are completed, the process continues with the completion of the production. After production is completed, a quality check is performed.

Once the quality check is done, a quality report is created. After the quality report is created, another decision is made. If adjustments are needed, the process loops back to this decision point until no further adjustments are required.

If adjustments are needed, another decision is made to determine the next step. If adjustments are still required, settings are adjusted, and the process returns to the previous decision point. If no adjustments are needed, data is sent to the warehouse, and the process returns to the previous decision point.

Once it is determined that no further adjustments are needed, the process comes to an end.
