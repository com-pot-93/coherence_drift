The process begins when a customer order is received. After receiving the order, the next step is to pick the items from stock that are needed to fulfill the order. Once the items have been picked, they are packed into parcels. The packed parcels are then weighed.

After weighing the parcels, shipping labels are printed for each parcel. At this point, three activities are carried out at the same time: the parcels are moved to the loading dock, the number of parcels is checked, and the quality of the packing is inspected. These three activities happen independently and simultaneously.

Once all three of these activities are completed, the process continues with two more sets of activities that also happen at the same time. The first set involves loading the parcels onto a truck and then sending delivery instructions to the transporter. The second set involves updating the order status and then adjusting the stock levels to reflect the items that have been shipped.

After both sets of activities are finished, the process comes to an end.
