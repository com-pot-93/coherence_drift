The process begins when a customer order is received. Once the order is in hand, the next step is to pick the required items from stock. After the items have been picked, they are packed into parcels. These parcels are then weighed to ensure accuracy.

Following the weighing, shipping labels are printed for the parcels. At this point, three activities are carried out at the same time: the parcels are moved to the loading dock, the number of parcels is checked, and the quality of the packing is inspected. All three of these actions happen in parallel and must be completed before the process continues.

Once these parallel activities are finished, the process splits again into two sets of actions that also occur at the same time. The first set involves loading the parcels onto a truck and sending delivery instructions to the transporter. The second set involves updating the order status and adjusting the stock levels to reflect the items that have been shipped. Both sets of actions must be completed before the process comes to an end.

The process concludes after all these steps have been carried out.
