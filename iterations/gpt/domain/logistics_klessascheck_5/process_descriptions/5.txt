The process begins when the product is ready to be handled. The first step is to package the product. Once the product is packaged, it is shipped.

After shipping, a decision is made about how the product will be delivered. There are two possible delivery methods:

1. The product is delivered directly to the recipient’s door.
2. The product is delivered to a designated station.

If the product is delivered to the door, another decision is made to determine whether a receipt is needed:

- If a receipt is needed, a pick-up receipt is printed. After printing, the receipt is posted, and then the product is rerouted to the station. Once rerouted, the process continues to the next stage.
- If no receipt is needed, the process moves directly to the next stage without printing or posting a receipt.

If the product is delivered to the station, the process also moves to the next stage.

At this point, regardless of the previous path, the process checks whether the product has been received back:

- If the product has been received back, it is processed accordingly.
- If the product has not been received back, the process continues without this step.

Finally, the process reaches its last step, after which it ends.
