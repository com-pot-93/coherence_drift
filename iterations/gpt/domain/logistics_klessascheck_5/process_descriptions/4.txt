The process begins with the start of the procedure. The first step is to package the product. Once the product is packaged, it is shipped.

After shipping, a decision is made about how to deliver the product. There are two possible delivery methods:

1. **Deliver to Door:**  
   If the product is to be delivered directly to the recipient’s door, the process continues with the delivery to the door. After this, another decision is made regarding whether a receipt is needed:
   - If a receipt is needed, a pick-up receipt is printed. The receipt is then posted, and the product is rerouted to a station. After rerouting, the process continues to the next decision point.
   - If no receipt is needed, the process skips the receipt steps and proceeds directly to the next decision point.

2. **Deliver to Station:**  
   If the product is to be delivered to a station instead of the door, the process continues with the delivery to the station. After this, the process moves to the same decision point as above.

At this decision point, it is determined whether the product has been received back:
- If the product has been received back, the received product is processed. After processing, the process moves to the final step.
- If the product has not been received back, the process skips the processing step and moves directly to the final step.

The process concludes with the completion of the final step, marking the end of the procedure.
