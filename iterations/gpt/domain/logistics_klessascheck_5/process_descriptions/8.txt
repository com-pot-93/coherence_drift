The process begins with the start of the procedure. The first step is to package the product. Once the product is packaged, it is shipped.

After shipping, a decision is made about how to deliver the product. There are two possible delivery methods:

1. **Deliver to Door:**  
   If the product is to be delivered to the recipient’s door, the process continues with the delivery to the door. After this, another decision is made regarding whether a receipt is needed:
   - If a receipt is needed, a pick-up receipt is printed, then the receipt is posted, and the product is rerouted to a station.  
   - If no receipt is needed, the process skips directly to the next step.

   In both cases, the process continues to the next decision point.

2. **Deliver to Station:**  
   If the product is to be delivered to a station, it is delivered there, and the process moves to the same decision point as above.

At this stage, a check is made to determine if the product has been received back:
- If the product has been received back, it is processed accordingly.
- If the product has not been received back, the process continues without this step.

Finally, the process moves to the last step, which is the final step of the procedure. Once this is completed, the process ends.
