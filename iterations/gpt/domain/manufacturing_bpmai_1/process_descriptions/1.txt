The process begins with the creation of a production plan and a process order. Once this is completed, the next step is to carry out material requirements planning to determine what materials are needed for production. After the material requirements have been planned, the available capacity is evaluated to ensure that resources are sufficient for the planned production.

Following the capacity evaluation, a proposal is scheduled and released. Once the proposal is released, the process order is confirmed. At this point, there is a decision to be made between two possible actions: either a transfer order is created, or a goods issue is recorded.

If a transfer order is created, the process continues to the next step. Alternatively, if a goods issue is recorded, the process also moves forward to the same next step. After either the transfer order is created or the goods issue is recorded, the goods issue is confirmed.

Once the goods issue has been confirmed, the receipt of the finished goods is recorded. After recording the receipt of the finished goods, the order is settled. The process concludes after the order has been settled.
