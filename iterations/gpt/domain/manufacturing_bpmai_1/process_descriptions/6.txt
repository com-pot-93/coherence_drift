The process begins with the start of the workflow. The first step is to create a production plan. Once the production plan is ready, the next step is to create a process order. After the process order has been created, material requirements planning is performed to ensure all necessary materials are available.

Following this, the available capacity is evaluated to determine if the resources needed for production are sufficient. Once capacity has been assessed, a proposal for scheduling and releasing the order is prepared. After the proposal is ready, the process order is confirmed.

At this point, a decision is made. If a transfer order is needed, a transfer order is created. If the goods issue has already been recorded, the process skips the creation of the transfer order. In both cases, the process continues to the next step.

The next step is to record the goods issue, which documents the withdrawal of materials for production. After the goods issue is recorded, it is confirmed to ensure accuracy. The process then proceeds to record the receipt of finished goods, acknowledging that the production output has been received.

Finally, the order is settled, completing all financial and logistical activities related to the process. The process ends at this point.
