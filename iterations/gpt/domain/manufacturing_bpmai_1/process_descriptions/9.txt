The process begins with the creation of a production plan. Once the production plan is created, the next step is to create a process order. After the process order is created, material requirements planning is performed to ensure that all necessary materials are available for production.

Following this, the available capacity is evaluated to determine if the resources required for production are sufficient. Once capacity has been evaluated, a proposal for scheduling and releasing the order is prepared. After the proposal is ready, the process order is confirmed.

At this point, a decision is made. If a transfer order is needed, a transfer order is created. If the goods issue has already been recorded, the process skips the creation of a transfer order. In either case, the process continues to the next step, which is recording the goods issue.

After the goods issue is recorded, the goods issue is confirmed. The next step is to record the receipt of the finished goods. Once the finished goods have been received, the order is settled. The process ends after the order has been settled.
