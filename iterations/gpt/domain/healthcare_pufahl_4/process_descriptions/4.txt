The process begins when the patient’s condition is assessed. After this initial assessment, two activities are started at the same time:

- A physical examination of the patient is performed.
- Blood samples are drawn from the patient.

Once the blood samples have been drawn, they are sent to the laboratory. The laboratory then processes the samples, and the results are received.

The process waits until both the physical examination is completed and the laboratory results have been received. Only after both are finished does the process continue.

Next, a diagnosis is made based on the information gathered. After the diagnosis, there is a decision point:

- If surgery has already been performed, the patient is discharged.
- If surgery is planned, preparations for surgery are made.

After either discharging the patient or planning the surgery, the process comes to an end.
