The process begins when the patient’s condition is assessed. After this initial assessment, two activities are carried out at the same time: a physical examination is performed, and blood samples are drawn from the patient.

While the physical examination is being conducted, the blood samples are sent to the laboratory. In the laboratory, the samples are processed, and then the laboratory results are received.

Once both the physical examination is completed and the laboratory results have been received, the process continues with making a diagnosis.

After the diagnosis is made, there are two possible paths depending on the outcome:

- If surgery has already been performed, the patient is discharged.
- If surgery is planned, preparations for surgery are made.

Regardless of which path is taken, the process ends after either the patient is discharged or preparations for surgery are completed.
