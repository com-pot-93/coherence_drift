The process begins when the patient’s conditions are assessed. After this initial assessment, two activities are carried out at the same time:

- A physical examination is performed.
- Blood samples are drawn from the patient, which are then sent to the laboratory for analysis. Once the laboratory completes the analysis, the results are received.

Once both the physical examination and the laboratory analysis results are available, the process continues with making a diagnosis.

After the diagnosis is made, a decision is required. Depending on the outcome, one of two actions is taken:

- If appropriate, a surgical intervention is performed.
- Alternatively, surgery is planned.

Regardless of which of these two actions is taken, the process then proceeds to discharging the patient.

Finally, the process ends after the patient is discharged.
