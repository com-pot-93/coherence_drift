The process begins when a data subject request is received. After receiving the request, the next step is to check whether the data involved is relevant.

Once the relevance of the data is checked, a decision is made:

- If the data is considered relevant, the process continues by stopping the use of the withdrawn data. After this, the withdrawal is communicated to the appropriate parties. Then, the process waits for any further actions that may be required. Once this waiting period is over, the process moves towards completion.

- If the data is not considered relevant, the user is informed about the impact of their request. After informing the user, the process waits for a notification. When the notification is received, another decision is made:

    - If the decision is to stop business activities, the process proceeds to stop the business activities, then stops the use of the withdrawn data, and communicates the withdrawal. After this, the process moves towards completion.
    
    - If the decision is not to stop business activities, the process moves directly towards completion.

The process ends after all necessary actions and communications have been completed, depending on the path taken.
