The process begins when a request from a data subject is received. After receiving this request, the next step is to check whether the data that is being withdrawn is relevant.

Based on the outcome of this check, the process can follow one of two paths:

**First Path:**
- If the withdrawn data is relevant, the process proceeds to stop using the withdrawn data.
- After stopping the use of this data, the next step is to communicate the withdrawal.
- Following this communication, a decision is made. Regardless of the outcome of this decision, the process continues to the next decision point, and then moves forward to a final decision point before the process ends.

**Second Path:**
- If the withdrawn data is not immediately relevant, the user is informed that the withdrawal will stop all running business processes.
- The process then waits to receive a notification about whether to continue.
- Once this notification is received, another decision is made:
    - If the decision is to stop, all running business processes that use the withdrawn data are stopped.
    - After stopping these processes, the process continues to stop using the withdrawn data.
    - Then, the withdrawal is communicated.
    - After this, another decision is made. Regardless of the outcome, the process continues to a further decision point, and then to a final decision point before ending.
    - If the decision after receiving the notification is not to stop, the process moves directly to the next decision point, and then to the final decision point before ending.

In both main paths, after passing through the final decision point, the process concludes.
