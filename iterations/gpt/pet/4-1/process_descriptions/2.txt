The process begins when a notice is received at the secretarial office. After receiving the notice, the next step is to ask for the patient’s data. Once the patient data is obtained, the doctor is connected to the nursing officer. The nursing officer then inquires about the patient’s condition.

Following this, the information gathered is recorded on a registration form. The completed registration form is handed back to the secretarial office. The secretarial office then enters the information into the information system. After entering the data, the information from the form is printed.

At this point, a decision is made about whether a patient file is required. If a file is required, a patient file is created, and the registration form along with the printout is stored in the patient file. If a file is not required, the process continues without creating a file.

Next, two registration cards are produced. The new patient is then added to the list of new notices. A staff meeting is held, during which new patients are assigned to team members. These assignments are recorded on an assignment list.

Another decision is made to determine if a medical file is needed. If a medical file is needed, a decision is made regarding the medical file. If not, the process continues. In both cases, information is added to the assignment list, and the assignment information is stored in the information system.

The registration cards are then distributed. At this stage, it is determined whether a letter to the family doctor is needed. If a letter is needed, it is prepared and sent to the family doctor. If no letter is needed, the process continues.

After this, a copy of the patient’s medical file is received. The responsible intake person is informed, and the received copy is added to the patient file. The first intake worker then plans a meeting with the patient and examines the patient. Any additional observations are recorded in notes, and a copy of these notes is placed in the patient file. A standard checklist is also added to the patient file.

A decision is made about whether a second intake is needed. If a second intake is needed, the second intake worker plans the first meeting. If not, the process continues.

Next, recorded tapes from the meeting are typed out, and the typed notes are added to the patient file. The patient is then put on the list of patients. This list is provided to the team leader. Finally, the team members create a treatment plan for the patient, concluding the process.
