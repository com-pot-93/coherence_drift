Certainly! Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process as depicted in your model:

1. The process begins when a customer makes a notice at the secretarial office.
2. After receiving the notice, the next step is to ask for the patient’s data.
3. Once the patient’s data is collected, the process continues by connecting the doctor to the nursing officer.
4. The nursing officer then inquires about the patient’s condition.
5. The information gathered is recorded on a registration form.
6. The completed registration form is handed back to the secretarial office.
7. The secretarial office enters the information from the form into the information system.
8. After entering the data, the information from the form is printed out.
9. At this point, a decision is made about whether a file is required for the patient:
    - If a file is required, a patient file is created.
    - The registration form and the printout are then stored in the patient file.
    - The process continues to the next step.
    - If a file is not required, the process skips directly to the next step.
10. Two registration cards are produced.
11. The new patient is added to the list of new notices.
12. A staff meeting is held where the team gathers.
13. During the meeting, new patients are assigned to team members.
14. The assignments are recorded on an assignment list.
15. Another decision is made about whether a medical file is needed:
    - If a medical file is needed, a decision is made regarding the medical file.
    - The process continues to the next step.
    - If a medical file is not needed, the process skips directly to the next step.
16. Information is added to the assignment list.
17. The assignment information is stored in the information system.
18. The registration cards are distributed.
19. Next, a decision is made about whether a letter to the family doctor is needed:
    - If a letter is needed, a letter to the family doctor is prepared and then sent.
    - The process continues to the next step.
    - If no letter is needed, the process skips directly to the next step.
20. A copy of the patient’s medical file is received.
21. The person responsible for intake is informed.
22. The received copy is added to the patient file.
23. The first intake worker plans a meeting with the patient.
24. The first intake worker examines the patient.
25. Any additional observations from the examination are recorded in notes.
26. A copy of these notes is placed in the patient file.
27. A standard checklist is added to the patient file.
28. Another decision is made about whether a second intake is needed:
    - If a second intake is needed, the second intake worker plans the first meeting.
    - The process continues to the next step.
    - If no second intake is needed, the process skips directly to the next step.
29. The recorded tapes from the meeting are typed out.
30. The information from the typed notes is added to the patient file.
31. The patient is put on the list of patients.
32. The team leader is provided with the list of patients.
33. The team members create a treatment plan for the patient.
34. The process ends.
