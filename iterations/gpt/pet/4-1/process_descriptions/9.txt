The process begins when a notice is received at the secretarial office. After receiving the notice, the secretarial office requests patient data. The necessary patient data is then obtained. Once the data is available, the process continues by connecting the doctor to the nursing officer.

The nursing officer then inquires about the patient. The information gathered is recorded on a registration form. This completed registration form is returned to the secretarial office. The secretarial office enters the information from the form into the information system and prints out the information from the form.

At this point, a decision is made about whether a file is required for the patient. If a file is required, a patient file is created, and both the registration form and the printout are stored in the file. If no file is required, the process skips directly to the next step.

Next, two registration cards are produced for the patient. The patient is then added to a list of new notices. A staff meeting is held to assign patients, and the assignments are recorded on an assignment list.

Another decision is made to determine if a medical file is needed. If a medical file is needed, a decision is made about the medical file. If no medical file is needed, the process continues without this step.

After this, the assignment information is added to the information system. The registration cards are then distributed.

A further decision is made about whether a letter needs to be sent to the family doctor. If a letter is needed, it is prepared and sent. If no letter is needed, the process continues.

Next, a copy of the patient’s medical file is received. The responsible intake person is informed, and the copy of the medical file is added to the patient file. A meeting with the patient is planned, and the patient is examined. Any additional observations are recorded in notes, and a copy of these notes is added to the patient file. A standard checklist is also added to the patient file.

At this stage, a decision is made about whether a second intake is needed. If a second intake is needed, the second intake worker plans the first meeting. If no second intake is needed, the process continues.

After this, any recorded tapes are typed out, and the typed notes are added to the patient file. The patient is then put on a list of patients, and this list is provided to the team leader. Finally, a treatment plan is created, and the process ends.
