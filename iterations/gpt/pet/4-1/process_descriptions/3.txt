Certainly! Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process you provided:

1. The process begins when a notice is received at the secretarial office.
2. The secretarial office then requests patient data.
3. After obtaining the necessary data, the office connects the doctor to the nursing officer.
4. The nursing officer inquires about the patient’s condition.
5. The information gathered is recorded on a registration form.
6. The completed registration form is returned to the secretarial office.
7. The secretarial office enters the information from the form into the information system.
8. The information from the form is printed out.
9. At this point, a decision is made about whether a patient file is required:
    - If a file is required, a patient file is created.
    - The registration form and the printout are stored in the patient file.
10. If no file is required, or after storing the documents in the file, the process continues.
11. Two registration cards are produced.
12. The new patient is added to the list of new notices.
13. A staff meeting is held.
14. During the meeting, new patients are assigned to team members.
15. The assignments are recorded on an assignment list.
16. Another decision is made about whether a medical file is needed:
    - If a medical file is needed, a decision is made regarding the medical file.
17. If no medical file is needed, or after the decision, the process continues.
18. The relevant information is added to the assignment list.
19. The assignment information is stored in the information system.
20. The registration cards are distributed.
21. Next, a decision is made about whether a letter to the family doctor is needed:
    - If a letter is needed, it is prepared and sent to the family doctor.
22. If no letter is needed, or after sending the letter, the process continues.
23. A copy of the patient’s medical file is received.
24. The responsible intake person is informed.
25. The received copy is added to the patient file.
26. A meeting with the patient is planned.
27. The patient is examined.
28. Any additional observations are recorded in notes.
29. A copy of these notes is added to the patient file.
30. A standard checklist is also added to the patient file.
31. Another decision is made about whether a second intake is needed:
    - If a second intake is needed, the second intake worker plans the first meeting.
32. If no second intake is needed, or after planning the meeting, the process continues.
33. Recorded tapes are typed out.
34. The typed notes are added to the patient file.
35. The patient is put on the list of patients.
36. This list is provided to the team leader.
37. Finally, a treatment plan is created, and the process ends.
