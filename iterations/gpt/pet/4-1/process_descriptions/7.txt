The process begins when a notice is received at the secretarial office. After receiving the notice, the secretarial office requests patient data. The necessary patient data is then obtained. Once the data is available, the doctor is connected to the nursing officer. The nursing officer inquires about the patient’s condition and records the information on a registration form. This completed registration form is returned to the secretarial office.

Next, the information from the registration form is entered into the information system. Afterward, the information is printed from the form. At this point, a decision is made about whether a file is required for the patient. If a file is required, a patient file is created, and both the form and the printout are stored in the file. If no file is required, this step is skipped.

Regardless of whether a file was created, two registration cards are produced. The new patient is then added to the list of new notices. A staff meeting is held to assign patients, and the assignments are recorded on an assignment list.

Another decision is made about whether a medical file is needed. If a medical file is needed, a decision is made about the medical file. If not, this step is skipped. After this, assignment information is added to the information system, and the registration cards are distributed.

A further decision is made about whether a letter needs to be sent to the family doctor. If a letter is needed, it is prepared and sent. If not, this step is skipped. Afterward, a copy of the patient’s medical file is received.

The responsible intake person is then informed, and the copy of the medical file is added to the patient file. A meeting with the patient is planned, and the patient is examined. Any additional observations are recorded in notes, and a copy of these notes is added to the patient file. A standard checklist is also added to the patient file.

At this stage, it is determined whether a second intake is needed. If a second intake is needed, the second intake worker plans the first meeting. If not, this step is skipped. Next, recorded tapes are typed out, and the typed notes are added to the patient file.

The patient is then put on the list of patients, and this list is provided to the team leader. Finally, a treatment plan is created, and the process ends.
