The process begins when the MPON reports the operation of a meter to the GO. After receiving this report, the GO examines the application. Based on this examination, the GO makes a decision: the application can either be confirmed or rejected.

If the GO confirms the application, the process continues with the GO confirming the application. If the GO rejects the application, the process continues with the GO rejecting the application. Regardless of whether the application is confirmed or rejected, the next step is the same: the GO informs the MPOO about the registration confirmation. After this, the GO also informs the MSPO about the registration confirmation.

Once both the MPOO and MSPO have been informed, two activities happen at the same time: the MPON acquires or changes equipment, and the MPOO acquires or changes equipment. Both of these actions must be completed before the process continues.

After both equipment-related actions are finished, the MPON informs the GO about the outcome. At this point, the GO evaluates the outcome and considers whether it was a failure or a success.

If the outcome is a failure, the GO informs the MPON about the failure. Then, the GO confirms the failure of the assignment. If the outcome is a success, the process skips these two steps.

Regardless of whether the outcome was a failure or a success, the next step is for the GO to assign the MPON and confirm the assignment. After this, three notifications are sent out at the same time: the GO informs the MPOO about the assignment status, the GO informs the MSPO about the assignment status, and the GO informs the SP about the assignment status. Once all three notifications have been sent, the process ends.
