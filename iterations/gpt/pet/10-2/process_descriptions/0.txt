The process begins when the MPON reports the meter operation to the GO. After receiving this report, the GO examines the application. Based on this examination, the GO makes a decision:

- If the application is rejected, the GO rejects the application. Regardless of this rejection, the process continues to the next step.
- If the application is confirmed, the GO confirms the application. The process then continues in the same way as after a rejection.

After either rejecting or confirming the application, the GO informs the MPOO about the registration confirmation. Following this, the GO also informs the MSPO about the registration confirmation.

Next, the MPON and the MPOO work together to perform the equipment acquisition and/or make any necessary equipment changes. Once this is done, there are two possible outcomes:

- If there is a failure, the MPON informs the GO about the failure. The GO then decides how to respond:
    - The GO may inform the MPON about the failure.
    - Alternatively, the GO may confirm the failure of the assignment.
  After either informing the MPON or confirming the failure, the process continues to the next step.
- If the equipment acquisition and/or changes are completed successfully, the MPON informs the GO about the successful completion. The GO then assigns the MPON and confirms this assignment.

After handling either the failure or the successful completion, the GO informs the MPOO about the assignment status of the MPON. Then, the GO informs the MSPO about the assignment status of the MPON. Finally, the GO informs the SP about the assignment status of the MPON, and the process ends.
