The process begins. At the start, there is a decision point to determine who initiates the process. If the process is started by an external party, the external party submits a change request. If the process is started by the MPO, the MPO begins the change process. Regardless of who initiates, the process continues to the next step.

Next, the MPO reviews the change request. After the review, there is another decision point. If the MPO decides to reject the change request, the MPO rejects the change request, and the process moves forward. If the MPO decides to confirm the change request, the MPO confirms the change request, and the process also moves forward.

After either rejecting or confirming the change request, the process continues with the MPO carrying out the change. Once the change is carried out, there is a final decision point to determine the outcome of the change.

If the change is successful, a report of successful implementation is created. If the change fails, a notification of the change failure is sent. After either reporting success or notifying failure, the process ends.
