The process begins with a starting point. From here, there are two possible ways the process can be initiated:

1. The process may be started by an external party. In this case, the external party submits a change request.
2. Alternatively, the process may be started by the MPO (Metropolitan Planning Organization), who begins the change process directly.

Regardless of how the process is started, the next step is for the MPO to review the change request.

After the review, there are two possible outcomes:

- If the MPO decides to reject the request, the MPO formally rejects it.
- If the MPO decides to confirm the request, the MPO confirms it.

In either case, after the request is either rejected or confirmed, the process continues with the MPO carrying out the change.

Once the MPO has carried out the change, there are again two possible outcomes:

- If the change is successful, the MPO reports the successful implementation.
- If the change fails, the MPO notifies the relevant parties about the failure.

After either reporting the success or notifying about the failure, the process comes to an end.
