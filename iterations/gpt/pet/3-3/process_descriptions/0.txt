The process begins when the system registers a claim. After this, a claims officer examines the claim. Once the examination is complete, a decision point is reached.

At this decision point, if the process is to continue, the claims officer writes a settlement recommendation. This recommendation is then checked by a senior claims officer. After checking, the senior claims officer marks the claim.

Following this, another decision point occurs. At this stage, there are two possible actions:

- The system may send the claim back. If this happens, the process returns to the earlier decision point after the claim examination, allowing the process to repeat from that stage.
- Alternatively, the system may proceed with the claim handling process. If this happens, the process also returns to the earlier decision point after the claim examination, again allowing for repetition.

At the earlier decision point, if the process does not continue with writing a settlement recommendation, the process ends.

In summary, the process involves registering and examining a claim, making decisions about how to proceed, possibly writing and checking a settlement recommendation, and either sending the claim back for further review or proceeding with handling, with the possibility of repeating certain steps until the process is concluded.
