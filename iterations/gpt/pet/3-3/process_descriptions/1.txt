The process begins when a claim is registered. After the claim is registered, it is examined. Once the examination is complete, a decision point is reached.

At this decision point, the process checks whether to continue with the next steps or to end the process. If the process continues, a settlement recommendation is written. After the recommendation is written, it is checked. Following this, the claim is marked.

Next, another decision point is reached. Here, there are two possible actions:

- If the claim needs to be sent back, it is sent back for further review. After being sent back, the process returns to the earlier decision point after the claim examination.
- If the process should proceed with handling the claim, the claim handling continues. After this, the process also returns to the earlier decision point after the claim examination.

If, at the earlier decision point after examining the claim, the process does not need to continue, the process ends.
