The process begins when a claim is registered. After the claim is registered, it is examined. Once the examination is complete, a decision point is reached.

At this decision point, the process checks whether to proceed further or not. If the decision is to proceed, a settlement recommendation is written. After the recommendation is written, it is checked. Following this, the claim is marked.

After marking the claim, another decision point occurs. Here, there are two possible outcomes:

- If the claim needs to be sent back for further review, it is sent back for further review. After this review, the process returns to the previous decision point after the claim examination.
- If the claim can proceed, the process continues with claim handling. After continuing the claim handling, the process also returns to the previous decision point after the claim examination.

If, at the initial decision point after examining the claim, the process does not need to proceed with writing a settlement recommendation, the process ends.

This cycle can repeat, allowing claims to be sent back for further review or to continue handling as needed, until the process reaches a point where it ends.
