The process begins when mail is collected from a party. After collecting the mail, it is sorted while still unopened. Once sorted, the mail is distributed to the intended recipients. The recipients then open the mail. After opening, the mail is sorted again. Next, the mail is registered in the Mail Register.

Following registration, a quality check is performed. At this point, a decision is made: if the mail requires a requisition, a requisition list is compiled and then sent to the party. After sending the requisition list, the process continues to the next step.

If the mail does not require a requisition, or after the requisition list has been sent, the process moves on to capturing the details of the matter. These details are then provided to the Cashier. The Cashier takes any applicable fees from the party.

After the fees are collected, two sets of activities happen at the same time. First, a receipt is put in its designated place, then documents are copied into an envelope, and finally, these documents are posted to the party. At the same time, the details of the party are captured, and a physical court file is printed.

Once both sets of activities are completed, the process comes together and ends.
