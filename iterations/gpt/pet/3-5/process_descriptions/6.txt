The process begins when mail is collected. After collecting the mail, it is sorted while still unopened. Once sorted, the mail is distributed to the intended recipients. The recipients then open the mail. After opening, the mail is sorted again. The details of the mail are then recorded in a register.

Next, a quality check is performed on the recorded mail details. At this point, a decision is made: if a requisition is required, a list of requisitions is compiled and then sent. If no requisition is required, the process continues without compiling or sending a requisition list. In both cases, the process proceeds to the next step.

After this, the details of the matter are captured. These details are then provided to the cashier. The cashier collects the necessary fees.

Once the fees are collected, two sets of activities happen at the same time. On one side, a receipt is placed in a designated location, then documents are copied into an envelope, and finally, these documents are posted to the relevant party. On the other side, the details of the party are captured, and a physical court file is printed. Both sets of activities are completed before the process comes to an end.
