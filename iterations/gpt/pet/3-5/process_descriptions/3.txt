The process begins when mail is collected from a party. After collecting the mail, it is sorted while still unopened. Once sorted, the mail is distributed to the intended recipients. The recipients then open the mail. After opening, the mail is sorted again. The details of the mail are then recorded in a register.

Next, a quality check is performed on the recorded mail. At this point, a decision is made: if a requisition is required, a requisition list is compiled and then sent. If no requisition is needed, the process continues without compiling or sending a requisition list.

After the requisition decision (and sending the list if needed), the process continues by capturing the details of the matter at hand. These details are then provided to the cashier. The cashier collects the necessary fees.

Once the fees are collected, two sets of activities happen at the same time. On one side, a receipt is placed in a designated location, and then documents are copied into an envelope. These documents are then posted to the party. On the other side, the details of the party are captured, and a physical court file is printed.

After both sets of activities are completed, the process comes to an end.
