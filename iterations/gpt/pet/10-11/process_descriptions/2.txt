The process begins when GO starts by making a measurement request. After this, MSP receives the request and reviews it.

At this point, MSP decides whether to approve or deny the request. If MSP decides to deny the request, MSP formally denies it and then informs GO about the denial. After GO is informed, the process moves towards completion.

If MSP does not deny the request, MSP proceeds to perform the measurement. Once the measurement is performed, MSP checks if the measurement was successful or not. If the measurement fails, MSP notifies GO about the failure, and the process moves towards completion.

If the measurement is successful, MSP sends the measured values to GO. GO then processes these measured values. After processing, GO checks whether any of the values have changed. If GO finds that the values have changed, GO sends the changed values back to MSP. If there are no changes, this step is skipped.

In both cases—whether GO sends changed values to MSP or not—GO then forwards the readings to SP. After this, the process moves towards completion.

The process ends after all these steps are completed, regardless of whether the request was denied, the measurement failed, or the readings were successfully processed and forwarded.
