The process begins when the GO initiates a request for measurements from the MSP. After receiving this request, the MSP reviews it to decide how to proceed.

At this point, there are two possible outcomes:

- If the MSP decides to deny the request, the MSP formally denies it and then informs the GO about this denial. The process then moves towards its conclusion.
- If the MSP does not deny the request, the MSP proceeds to perform the measurement as requested.

Once the measurement is attempted, there are again two possible outcomes:

- If the measurement fails, the MSP notifies the GO about the failure. The process then moves towards its conclusion.
- If the measurement is successful, the MSP sends the measured values to the GO.

Upon receiving the measured values, the GO processes them. After processing, the GO checks whether any of the values have changed:

- If the values have changed, the GO sends the changed values back to the MSP.
- If there are no changes, this step is skipped.

In either case, the GO then forwards the readings to the SP. After this, the process moves towards its conclusion.

Finally, the process ends.
