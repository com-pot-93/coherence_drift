The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there is a decision point where one of three approaches is chosen for acquiring customer contact information:

- If the "Cold Call" approach is selected, contact details are purchased.
- If the "Exhibition" approach is chosen, contacts are collected at an exhibition.
- If the "Existing Contact" approach is used, an already interested contact is utilized.

Regardless of which approach is taken, the process continues in the same way. The next step is to call the customer. After making the call, the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization is determined. Once the right contact person is identified, the next step is to find out the customer’s budget.

At this point, there is another decision:

- If the process is to proceed with sales, sales or pre-sales activities are carried out.
- Alternatively, if the head of development is to acquire the customer, the head of development takes over the acquisition.

After either of these actions, the process comes to an end.
