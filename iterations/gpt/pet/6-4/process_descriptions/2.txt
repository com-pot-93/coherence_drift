The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there are three possible ways to obtain their contact information:

- If the contact information is to be purchased, the process continues with purchasing contact details specifically for making cold calls.
- If the contacts are to be collected at exhibitions, the process proceeds with collecting contact information at such events.
- If there is already an existing interested contact, the process uses this existing contact information.

Regardless of which method is chosen to obtain the contact information, the process then continues in the same way. The next step is to call the customer. After making the call, the process involves determining the appropriate person to contact within the customer’s organization. Once the right contact person has been identified, the next step is to find out the customer’s budget.

Following this, the process assesses the customer’s level of interest. Based on this assessment, there are two possible paths:

- If it is appropriate to proceed with sales or pre-sales activities, the process continues with these activities.
- If the head of development needs to acquire the customer, the process moves to that step, where the head of development takes over the acquisition.

After either of these actions is completed, the process comes to an end.
