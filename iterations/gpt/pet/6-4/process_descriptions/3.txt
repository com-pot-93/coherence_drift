The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there are three possible ways to obtain their contact information:

1. If the contact information is to be purchased, the process continues with purchasing contact details specifically for making cold calls.
2. If the contact information is to be collected at an exhibition, the process continues with collecting contact information during the exhibition.
3. If there is already an existing interested contact, the process continues by using this existing contact.

After any of these three methods are used to obtain contact information, the process continues in the same way. The next step is to call the customer. Following the call, the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization is determined. After identifying the right person, the process moves on to finding out the customer’s budget.

At this point, there are two possible paths:

1. If the process is to proceed with sales, the next step is to carry out sales or pre-sales activities.
2. If the head of development is to acquire the customer, the process continues with the head of development taking over the acquisition of the customer.

After either of these two actions is completed, the process comes to an end.
