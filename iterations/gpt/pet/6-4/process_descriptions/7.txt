The process begins with the identification of potential customers. After this, a decision is made about how to proceed with acquiring customer contacts. There are three possible ways to move forward:

1. If the approach is to make a cold call, the next step is to purchase contact details.
2. If the approach is to attend an exhibition, the next step is to collect contacts at the exhibition.
3. If there is already an existing interested contact, that contact is used directly.

Regardless of which method is chosen, the process continues in the same way. The next step is to call the customer. After calling the customer, the process involves determining the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization. Once the contact person is identified, the next step is to find out the customer’s budget.

At this point, another decision is made about how to proceed:

1. If the process is to continue with sales, sales or pre-sales activities are carried out.
2. Alternatively, the head of development may take over and acquire the customer.

After either of these actions is completed, the process comes to an end.
