The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there are three possible ways to proceed, depending on the situation:

1. If the approach is through a cold call, the next step is to purchase contact details for the potential customer.
2. If the approach is through an exhibition, the next step is to collect contact information at the exhibition.
3. If there is already an existing interested contact, the process continues by using this existing contact.

Regardless of which of these three paths is taken, the process then continues in the same way. The next step is to call the customer. After making the call, the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization is determined. Once the right contact person has been identified, the next step is to find out the customer’s budget.

At this point, there are two possible ways to proceed:

1. If the sales process is to continue as usual, sales or pre-sales activities are carried out.
2. If the head of development is to take over, the head of development acquires the customer.

After either of these two actions is completed, the process comes to an end.
