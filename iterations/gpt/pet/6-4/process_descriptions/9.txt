The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once this is done, there are three possible ways to proceed, depending on how the contact with the customer is to be established:

- If the approach is through a cold call, the next step is to purchase contact details.
- If the approach is through an exhibition, the next step is to collect the contact information at the exhibition.
- If there is already an existing contact, the process continues by using this existing contact information.

Regardless of which of these three paths is taken, they all lead to the same next step: calling the customer. After the customer is called, the next step is to determine the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization. Once the correct contact person is identified, the process continues by finding out the customer’s budget.

At this point, there are two possible ways to proceed:

- If the process is to continue as usual, sales or pre-sales activities are carried out.
- If the head of development needs to take over, the head of development acquires the customer.

Both of these paths lead to the end of the process.
