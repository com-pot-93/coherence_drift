The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there are three possible ways to proceed:

1. If the approach is through cold calls, the next step is to purchase contact details.
2. If the approach is through an exhibition, the next step is to collect contacts at the exhibition.
3. If there is already an existing interested contact, the process continues by using this existing contact.

Regardless of which of these three paths is taken, the process then converges and continues with calling the customer. After the customer is called, the next step is to determine the appropriate contact person within the customer’s organization. Once the contact person is determined, the process moves on to finding out the customer’s budget.

At this point, there are two possible ways to proceed:

1. If the decision is to proceed with sales, the next step is to carry out sales or pre-sales activities.
2. Alternatively, if the head of development is to acquire the customer, the head of development takes over and acquires the customer.

After either of these actions is completed, the process comes to an end.
