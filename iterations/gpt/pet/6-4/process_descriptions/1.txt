The process begins with the identification of potential customers. Once potential customers have been identified, there are three possible ways to obtain their contact information:

1. If the contact information is to be purchased, the process continues by purchasing contact details specifically for making cold calls.
2. If the contact information is to be gathered at exhibitions, the process proceeds by collecting contacts during such events.
3. If there is already an existing interested contact, the process uses this known contact.

After any of these three approaches to obtaining contact information, the process continues by calling the customer. Following the call, the next step is to determine the appropriate person to contact within the customer’s organization. Once the correct contact person has been identified, the process moves on to finding out the customer’s budget.

After learning about the customer’s budget, the next step is to assess the customer’s level of interest. Based on this assessment, there are two possible paths:

- If the decision is to proceed with sales or pre-sales activities, the process moves directly towards completion.
- If the head of development is to acquire the customer, the process continues with the head of development taking responsibility for acquiring the customer, after which the process moves towards completion.

The process concludes after either proceeding with sales or pre-sales activities, or after the head of development has acquired the customer.
