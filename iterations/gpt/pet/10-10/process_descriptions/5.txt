The process begins with a starting point. Immediately, two activities are set to happen at the same time:

- The fault detector reports a failure.
- The MPO (Maintenance Process Owner) suspects a fault.

Once both of these actions are completed, the process continues. The MPO then examines the reported failure.

After examining the failure, the MPO must decide whether to reject or confirm the failure:

- If the MPO rejects the failure, the process moves directly towards its conclusion.
- If the MPO confirms the failure, the MPO takes several steps in sequence:
  1. The MPO informs the GO (presumably a relevant organizational unit) about the failure.
  2. The MPO informs the MSP (possibly a service provider) about the failure.
  3. The MPO fixes the fault.
  4. The MPO shares the results of the repair.
  5. The MPO informs the GO that the issue has been resolved.
  6. The MPO informs the MSP that the issue has been resolved.

After these steps, the process proceeds to its conclusion, just as it does if the failure was rejected. The process then ends.
