The process begins when an initial event occurs. Immediately, two activities happen at the same time: the Fault Detector reports a failure, and the MPO (Maintenance Process Owner) suspects a fault. Once both of these actions are completed, the process continues.

Next, the MPO examines the reported failure. After this examination, the MPO must decide whether to reject or confirm the failure.

If the MPO rejects the failure, the process moves directly towards its conclusion.

If the MPO confirms the failure, the MPO first informs the GO (presumably a relevant organizational role) about the failure. Then, the MPO informs the MSP (another relevant party) about the failure. After both parties have been informed, the MPO proceeds to fix the fault.

Once the fault has been fixed, the MPO shares the repair results. Following this, the MPO informs the GO that the issue has been resolved, and then informs the MSP that the issue has been resolved as well.

After either rejecting the failure or completing all the steps to resolve and communicate about the issue, the process comes to an end.
