Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process depicted in your model:

1. The process begins when an employee submits a request.
2. The request is then registered in the system.
3. After registration, the process reaches a decision point.
4. If the process continues, the request is sent for supervisor review.
5. The supervisor reviews the request, leading to another decision point.
6. If the supervisor does not approve the request, the request is returned with comments.
7. The employee reviews the supervisor’s comments.
8. The employee then decides whether to resubmit the request or withdraw it:
   - If the employee chooses to resubmit, the request goes back to the supervisor for another review.
   - If the employee chooses to withdraw, the process moves to a closing step.
9. After either resubmission or withdrawal, the process returns to the earlier decision point after supervisor review.
10. If the supervisor approves the request, it is forwarded to the purchase department.
11. The purchase department then reaches another decision point.
12. If the process continues, quotations are prepared.
13. After preparing quotations, another decision is made.
14. If a vendor is selected, the process moves forward to another decision point.
15. At this point, there are two options:
    - If the vendor is confirmed, the vendor is selected.
    - If a change of vendor is needed, the process returns to preparing quotations.
16. Once the vendor is selected, a purchase order is generated and sent.
17. The process then waits for product delivery.
18. After the product is delivered, an invoice is received.
19. The process checks if all steps are complete:
    - If all steps are complete, the user is notified of the status.
    - If not all steps are complete, the process moves to a series of checks and loops, which may return the process to earlier steps such as preparing quotations or selecting a vendor.
20. After notification or completion of necessary steps, the process eventually reaches its end.

Throughout the process, there are several points where decisions are made, and depending on the outcome, the process may loop back to earlier steps, such as preparing quotations, selecting a vendor, or reviewing the request. The process ensures that requests are reviewed, quotations are prepared, vendors are selected, and the employee is kept informed until the process is fully completed.
