The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

Following the review by the support officer, a decision is made about whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next stage. If modifications are needed, suggestions for changes are made, and then the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. After this review, the process moves forward.

Next, a check is performed to identify any schedule conflicts. Once this check is complete, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee. The committee then reviews the schedule.

After the committee’s review, another decision is made to determine if there are any conflicts. If no conflicts are found, the process proceeds to its conclusion. If conflicts are found, the coordination unit is informed about these conflicts, and then the process continues to its conclusion.

The process ends after these steps are completed.
