The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

At this point, a decision is made about whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next review stage. If modifications are needed, suggestions for changes are made, and then the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. After this review, the process moves forward.

Next, a check is performed to identify any schedule conflicts. If there are no issues, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee. The committee then reviews the schedule.

Following the committee’s review, another decision is made to determine if any conflicts have been found. If no conflicts are identified, the process proceeds towards completion. If conflicts are found, the coordination unit is informed about these conflicts, after which the process continues.

Finally, the process comes to an end.
