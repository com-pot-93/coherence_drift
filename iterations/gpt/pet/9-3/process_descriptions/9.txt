The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

Following the review by the support officer, a decision is made about whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next stage. If modifications are needed, suggestions for changes are made, and then the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. After this review, the process moves forward.

Next, a check is performed to identify any schedule conflicts. If no conflicts are found at this stage, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee. The committee then reviews the schedule, after which another check is conducted to see if any conflicts have arisen as a result of the committee’s review.

If no conflicts are found after the committee’s review, the process proceeds towards completion. If conflicts are found at this point, the coordination unit is informed about the committee’s conflicts, and then the process continues.

If, during the earlier check for schedule conflicts (before sending to the committee), conflicts are found, the coordination unit is informed about these conflicts, and the process continues.

Finally, after all necessary checks and notifications, the process comes to an end.
