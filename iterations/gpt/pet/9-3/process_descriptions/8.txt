The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

Following the review by the support officer, a decision is made about whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next stage. If modifications are needed, suggestions for changes are made, and then the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. After this review, the process moves forward.

Next, a check is performed to identify any schedule conflicts. If no conflicts are found, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee. The committee then reviews the schedule, and another check is performed to see if any conflicts have arisen after the committee’s review.

If no conflicts are found after the committee’s review, the process proceeds towards completion. However, if conflicts are found at this stage, the coordination unit is informed of the committee’s conflicts, and the process continues.

If, during the earlier check for schedule conflicts, any issues are found, the coordination unit is informed of these conflicts, and the process continues.

In all cases, after the necessary notifications and checks, the process concludes.
