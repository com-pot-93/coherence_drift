The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to the calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

Following the review by the support officer, a decision is made about whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next stage. If modifications are needed, suggestions for changes are made, and then the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. After this review, the process moves forward.

Next, a check is performed to identify any schedule conflicts. If no conflicts are found at this stage, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee. The committee then reviews the schedule. After the committee’s review, another check is made to determine if there are any conflicts.

If the committee finds no conflicts, the process proceeds towards completion. If the committee identifies conflicts, the coordination unit is informed about these conflicts. After the coordination unit is informed, the process continues and eventually rejoins the main flow.

If, during the earlier check for schedule conflicts, any conflicts are found, the coordination unit is also informed at that point. After being informed, the process continues and rejoins the main flow.

Finally, after all necessary reviews and conflict checks have been completed and any issues have been addressed, the process comes to an end.
