The process begins with the preparation of a draft schedule. Once the draft schedule is ready, the next step is to add the relevant dates to the calendars. After the dates have been added, a support officer reviews these dates.

At this point, the support officer decides whether any modifications are needed. If no modifications are required, the process continues directly to the next stage. If modifications are needed, the support officer suggests the necessary changes. After these suggestions are made, the coordination unit reviews the updated dates. Once this review is complete, the process moves forward.

Next, the schedule is checked for any conflicts. After this check, the finalized schedule is sent to the committee members. The committee members then review the schedule.

Following their review, the committee members determine if there are any conflicts. If no conflicts are found, the process proceeds to its conclusion. If conflicts are found, the committee members inform the coordination unit about these conflicts. After this notification, the process continues to its conclusion and ends.
