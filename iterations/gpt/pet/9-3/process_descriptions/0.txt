The process begins when the Coordination Unit prepares a draft schedule of meetings. After creating this draft, the Coordination Unit adds the proposed meeting dates to all relevant calendars. Once the dates have been added, the Support Officer reviews them to check for any issues.

If the Support Officer identifies any concerns or improvements, they suggest modifications to the schedule. The Coordination Unit then reviews all the dates again, taking into account the suggested changes. Following this, the Coordination Unit examines the schedule to identify any potential conflicts.

Once the schedule has been thoroughly checked and any conflicts have been addressed, the Coordination Unit sends the finalized schedule to all Committee Members. The Committee Members then review the schedule by checking their own diaries. If any Committee Member finds a conflict with the proposed dates, they inform the Coordination Unit about these conflicts.

The process concludes after the Committee Members have communicated any scheduling conflicts to the Coordination Unit.
