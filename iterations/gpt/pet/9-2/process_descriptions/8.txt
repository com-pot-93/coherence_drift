The process begins with the start of the procedure. The first step is to send a mortgage offer to the customer. After sending the offer, the process waits for a reply from the customer.

Once a reply is received, a decision is made. If the case needs to be updated, the process continues by updating the case details. After updating the case, the work is archived, and then a cancellation is performed. Following the cancellation, the process moves to another decision point.

If the case does not need to be updated, the process proceeds to perform administrative tasks. After these tasks are completed, another decision is made. If a request is needed, the process continues by requesting any outstanding documents from the customer. Once the documents are requested, the case details are updated, the work is archived, and a cancellation is performed. After the cancellation, the process moves to the next decision point.

If no request is needed after the administrative tasks, the process skips directly to the next decision point.

At the final decision point, regardless of the path taken—whether after updating the case and performing a cancellation, or after administrative tasks and any necessary requests and cancellations—the process comes together and then ends.
