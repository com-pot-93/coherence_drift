The process begins when a defective computer is received. First, the computer is examined to assess its condition. After the examination, the repair costs are calculated. These costs are then discussed with the customer.

At this point, the customer decides whether to proceed with the repair or not. If the customer declines the repair, the computer is returned to the customer, and the process moves towards completion.

If the customer approves the repair, the process continues with the repair activities. The repair work is divided into two parts that happen at the same time: one part involves checking and repairing the hardware, and the other part involves checking and configuring the software. Both of these tasks must be completed before moving forward.

Once both the hardware and software tasks are finished, the process checks if the computer is ready. If the computer is not ready, the process waits until it is ready, and then checks again.

When the computer is ready, the system’s functionality is tested. If the test is successful, the process proceeds towards completion. If the test fails, the issue is escalated, and the system functionality is tested again. This cycle repeats until the test is successful.

After a successful test, the process continues towards completion. Finally, the process ends.
