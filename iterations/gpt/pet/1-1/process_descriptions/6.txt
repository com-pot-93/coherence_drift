The process begins when an order is received. After receiving the order, a decision is made about whether to accept or reject it.

If the order is rejected, the process moves to handling the order rejection. Once the rejection is handled, the process comes to an end.

If the order is accepted, the storehouse is informed. After that, the engineering department is notified. Once both the storehouse and engineering department are informed, two activities happen at the same time: preparations for assembly begin, and the part list is processed.

While preparations for assembly are underway, the part list is checked, and then the quantities of the parts are verified. After verifying the part quantities, another decision is made:

- If all parts are available, the parts are reserved.
- If some parts are missing, a back-order is created.

After either reserving the parts or creating a back-order, the process continues. Once both the assembly preparations and the part list processing (including any necessary back-ordering or part reservation) are complete, the bicycle is assembled.

After the bicycle is assembled, it is shipped. Finally, the process ends.
