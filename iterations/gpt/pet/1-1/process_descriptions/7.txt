The process begins when an order is received. After receiving the order, a decision is made: if the order is to be rejected, the process moves to handling the order rejection. Once the rejection is handled, the process proceeds to the end.

If the order is not rejected, the storehouse is informed. After that, the engineering department is notified. Next, two activities are prepared to happen at the same time: assembly preparations begin, and the part list is processed.

For the assembly preparations, a decision point is reached, but regardless of the outcome, the process continues to the next step, where it waits for other activities to finish.

For the part list, after processing it, two activities are started at the same time: the part list is checked, and assembly preparations begin again. After checking the part list, the part quantities are verified. Then, a decision is made: if all parts are available, the parts are reserved; if some parts are missing, a back-order is created. After either reserving parts or creating a back-order, the process waits for the other activities to finish.

The assembly preparations that started in parallel also reach a decision point, but regardless of the outcome, the process continues and waits for the other activities to finish.

Once all parallel activities are completed, the process moves on to assembling the bicycle. After the bicycle is assembled, it is shipped. Finally, the process ends.
