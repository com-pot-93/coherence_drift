Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process as depicted in your model:

1. The process begins when a customer notices a decline in the quality of a service.
2. The customer sends the details of the problem to customer service.
3. Customer service receives this information and enters a problem report.
4. The problem report is compared with the existing service agreement.
5. Based on this comparison, the necessary countermeasures are determined.
6. At this point, a decision is made:
   - If no countermeasures are needed, a detailed problem report is created.
   - Otherwise, another decision is made with three possible paths:
     - The significance of the customer is assessed.
     - The issue is linked to an extra problem-fixing process.
     - The process proceeds directly without additional steps.
   - All these paths eventually lead to the creation of a detailed problem report.
7. The detailed problem report is sent to Service Management.
8. Service Management handles the quality violation.
9. The problem is investigated.
10. Another decision is made:
    - If an analysis is performed, an analysis is conducted and a trouble report is created.
    - If no analysis is performed, the process continues without this step.
11. The report is sent to Resource Provisioning.
12. Resource Provisioning receives the report.
13. A check for issues is performed.
14. Another decision is made:
    - If issues are found, there are three possible actions:
      - The problem is analyzed and a trouble report is created.
      - A notification about service execution is created.
      - It is determined that no further action is needed.
    - If no issues are found, the process continues.
    - All these paths eventually come together.
15. A status report is sent to Service Management.
16. Service Management prepares a final status report.
17. At this point, there are three possible actions:
    - If automatic restoration is requested:
      - Automatic restoration is requested.
      - Resource Provisioning tracks errors and corrective actions.
      - A troubleshooting report is created.
      - The troubleshooting report is sent to Service Management.
      - Service Management forwards the report to customer service.
      - Customer service creates a problem resolution report.
      - The problem resolution report is sent to customer service.
      - The report is received.
    - If actual service performance needs to be sent:
      - Actual service performance is sent to customer service.
      - The actual service performance is received.
    - If corrective actions are to be performed:
      - Corrective actions are performed.
      - A problem resolution report is created.
      - The problem resolution report is sent to customer service.
      - The report is received.
    - All these paths eventually come together.
18. Two activities are performed at the same time:
    - A report for the customer is created.
    - Any violation rebate is reported to billing, and billing adjusts the billing accordingly.
19. After both activities are completed, a report is sent to the customer.
20. The customer receives the report.
21. The process ends.
