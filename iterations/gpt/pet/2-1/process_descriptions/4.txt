Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process depicted in your model:

1. The process begins when a customer notices a decline in the quality of a service.
2. The customer sends the details of the problem to customer service.
3. A service employee receives this information and enters a problem report.
4. The service employee compares the problem report with the existing service agreement.
5. The necessary countermeasures are determined.
6. At this point, a decision is made:
   - If no countermeasures are needed, a detailed problem report is created.
   - If countermeasures are needed, another decision is made:
     - The significance of the customer is assessed.
     - The case may be linked to an extra problem-fixing process.
     - Alternatively, the process may proceed to the next step.
   - After any of these actions, the process continues.
7. The detailed problem report is sent to Service Management.
8. Service Management handles the quality violation.
9. Service Management investigates the problem.
10. Another decision is made:
    - If an analysis is performed, an analysis is conducted and a trouble report is created.
    - If no analysis is performed, the process continues.
11. The report is sent to Resource Provisioning.
12. Resource Provisioning receives the report.
13. A decision is made:
    - If there are issues, Resource Provisioning checks for them.
    - If there are no issues, the process continues.
14. If Resource Provisioning checks for issues, another decision is made:
    - The problem may be analyzed and a trouble report created.
    - A notification about service execution may be created.
    - No further action may be needed.
    - After any of these actions, the process continues.
15. The process continues and a status report is sent to Service Management.
16. Service Management prepares a final status report.
17. At this point, another decision is made:
    - If automatic restoration of resources is requested, the following steps occur:
      - A request for automatic restoration is made.
      - Resource Provisioning tracks errors and corrective actions.
      - A troubleshooting report is created.
      - The troubleshooting report is sent to Service Management.
      - Service Management forwards the report to customer service.
      - Customer service creates a problem resolution report.
      - The problem resolution report is sent to customer service.
      - The report is received.
    - If actual service performance needs to be sent, it is sent to customer service and received.
    - If corrective actions are to be performed, they are carried out, a problem resolution report is created, sent to customer service, and received.
18. After any of these paths, the process continues in parallel:
    - A report for the customer is created.
    - A violation rebate is reported to billing, and billing adjusts the billing accordingly.
19. Once both parallel actions are completed, a report is sent to the customer.
20. The customer receives the report.
21. The process ends.
