Certainly! Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process you provided:

1. The process begins when a customer notices a decline in the quality of a service.
2. The customer sends the details of the problem to the customer service team.
3. The customer service team receives the problem report and enters it into their system.
4. The problem is then compared with the existing service agreement to check if it falls under the agreed terms.
5. Based on this comparison, the necessary countermeasures are determined.
6. At this point, there are three possible paths:
   - If no countermeasures are needed, this is noted, and the process continues.
   - If further assessment is required, the significance of the customer is evaluated before proceeding.
   - If the issue needs to be linked to an extra problem-fixing process, this is done before moving on.
7. After any of these three actions, a detailed problem report is created.
8. This report is sent to the Service Management team.
9. The Service Management team handles the quality violation.
10. They then investigate the problem.
11. After the investigation, there are two possible outcomes:
    - If an analysis is performed, an analysis is conducted and a trouble report is created.
    - If no analysis is needed, the process continues without it.
12. The report is then sent to the Resource Provisioning team.
13. The Resource Provisioning team receives the report.
14. They check for any issues related to the resources.
15. At this stage, there are three possible actions:
    - If issues are found, the problem is analyzed and a trouble report is created.
    - If a notification about service execution is needed, it is created.
    - If no further action is required, the process continues.
16. After any of these actions, a status report is sent to the Service Management team.
17. The Service Management team prepares a final status report.
18. At this point, there are three possible next steps:
    - If automatic restoration is requested, this request is made. The Resource Provisioning team then tracks errors and corrective actions, creates a troubleshooting report, and sends it to Service Management. Service Management forwards the report to customer service, which creates a problem resolution report, sends it to customer service, and the customer service team receives the resolution report.
    - If actual service performance needs to be sent, this information is sent to customer service, and the customer service team receives the performance report.
    - If corrective actions need to be performed, these actions are carried out, a problem resolution report is created and sent to customer service, and the customer service team receives the resolution report.
19. After any of these three paths, the process continues with two activities happening at the same time:
    - A report for the customer is created.
    - Any violation rebate is reported to the billing department, which then adjusts the billing accordingly.
20. Once both activities are completed, the report is sent to the customer.
21. The customer receives the report.
22. The process ends.
