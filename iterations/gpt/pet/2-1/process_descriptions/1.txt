1. The process begins when a customer notices a decline in the quality of a service.
2. The system then sends the details of the problem to customer service.
3. A service employee enters a report about the problem.
4. The report is compared with the existing service agreement.
5. Based on this comparison, the necessary countermeasures are determined.
6. At this point, a decision is made about whether countermeasures are needed.
7. If countermeasures are needed, the process continues with a further decision that involves three possible actions:
    - The significance of the customer is determined.
    - The case is linked to an extra process for fixing the problem.
    - The process proceeds directly to the next step.
   All three of these actions eventually lead back to the main process flow.
8. If no countermeasures are needed, the process also continues along the main path.
9. Next, a detailed report about the problem is created.
10. This report is sent to Service Management.
11. Service Management takes responsibility for handling the quality violation.
12. Service Management investigates the problem.
13. At this stage, there is a choice between two actions:
    - An analysis is performed and a trouble report is created.
    - The report is sent to Resource Provisioning.
14. If the report is sent to Resource Provisioning, they check for any issues.
15. Resource Provisioning then chooses between two actions:
    - They analyze the problem and create a trouble report.
    - They create a notification about service execution.
   Both actions lead to the next step.
16. A status report is sent to Service Management.
17. The process continues with the preparation of a final status report.
18. At this point, there are three possible actions:
    - A request is created for automatic restoration of resources. Resource Provisioning receives this request, tracks errors and corrective actions, creates a troubleshooting report, sends it to Service Management, which then forwards it to customer service. Customer service creates a problem resolution report, sends it to customer service again, and finally receives the report.
    - The actual service performance is sent to customer service, which then receives the performance information.
    - Corrective actions are undertaken, a problem resolution report is created, sent to customer service, and received by customer service.
   All three actions eventually lead to the next step.
19. The process then splits into two activities that happen at the same time:
    - A report is created for the customer.
    - Any violation rebate is reported to billing, and billing adjusts the billing accordingly.
   Both activities are completed before moving on.
20. The report is sent to the customer.
21. The customer receives the report.
22. The process ends.
