Certainly! Here is a detailed, step-by-step description of the process as depicted in your model:

1. The process begins when a customer notices that the service quality has declined.
2. The system then sends a list of problem parameters to customer service.
3. A service employee enters a problem report into the system.
4. The service employee compares the problem report with the customer’s service agreement.
5. The service employee determines what countermeasures are necessary.
6. At this point, a decision is made:
   - If the decision is positive, the process continues to another decision point.
7. At the next decision point, there are three possible actions:
   - The electronic service determines how significant the customer is, and the process continues.
   - The service employee links the case to an extra problem fix process, and the process continues.
   - The process can also proceed directly to the next step.
8. After any of these actions, the process returns to the previous decision point, and then moves forward.
9. The service employee creates a detailed problem report.
10. The service employee sends the problem report to Service Management.
11. Service Management handles any violations of quality.
12. Service Management investigates the problem.
13. Another decision is made:
    - Service Management can either perform an analysis and create a trouble report, or send the problem report to Resource Provisioning.
14. If Service Management performs the analysis:
    - Service Management creates a trouble report, and the process continues.
15. If Service Management sends the problem report to Resource Provisioning:
    - Resource Provisioning checks for potential issues.
    - Another decision is made:
      - Resource Provisioning can analyze the problem and create a trouble report.
      - Or, Resource Provisioning can create a service execution notification.
    - After either action, Resource Provisioning sends a status report to Service Management, and the process continues.
16. After these steps, Service Management prepares the final status report.
17. At this point, there are three possible actions:
    - Service Management creates a request for automatic resource restoration, sends it to Resource Provisioning, and Resource Provisioning receives the request.
      - Resource Provisioning tracks all possible errors, undertakes all necessary corrective actions, creates a troubleshooting report, and sends it to Service Management.
      - Service Management sends the troubleshooting report to customer service, creates a problem resolution report, and sends it to customer service.
      - Customer service receives the problem resolution report.
    - Service Management sends the actual service performance to customer service, and customer service receives it.
    - Service Management undertakes corrective actions, creates a problem resolution report, sends it to customer service, and customer service receives the report.
18. After any of these actions, the process continues to the next stage, where two activities happen at the same time:
    - Customer service creates a report for the customer.
    - Customer service reports a service agreement violation rebate to billing and collections, and billing and collections adjusts the billing.
19. After both activities are completed, customer service sends the report to the customer.
20. The customer receives the report.
21. The process ends.
