The process begins when the company decides to go public. After making this decision, the company selects underwriters to assist with the process. The underwriters then prepare a registration statement, which is submitted for review.

Next, the registration statement is reviewed by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Once the SEC has reviewed the statement, the underwriters check to ensure that everything is in compliance with the necessary regulations.

At this point, the process splits into two activities that happen at the same time. On one side, the final issue price is determined, and then preparations are made for a road show to present the offering to potential investors. On the other side, the process waits for approval from the SEC.

Once both the road show has been arranged and the SEC approval has been received, the process continues. The company then receives clearance from the SEC. After receiving this clearance, the final issue price is set.

Following this, a firm commitment is made to purchase the stock. Finally, the stock is offered to the public, and the process comes to an end.
