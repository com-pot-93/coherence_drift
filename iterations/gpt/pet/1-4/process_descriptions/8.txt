The process begins with the initiation of the procedure. After starting, a decision is made about whether to proceed. If the decision is not to proceed, the process moves directly towards completion without any further actions.

If the decision is to continue, the next step is to select underwriters. Once the underwriters are chosen, a registration statement is prepared. This statement is then submitted for review. After submission, the registration statement is reviewed, followed by a check of compliance requirements.

Once compliance has been checked, three activities are carried out at the same time: determining the final issue price, preparing for the road show, and waiting for regulatory approval. These three activities are independent and can happen simultaneously.

After all three activities are completed, the process continues with receiving regulatory clearance. Once clearance is received, the final issue price is set. Following this, a firm commitment is made, and then the stock is offered to the public.

Finally, the process reaches another decision point, which leads directly to the end of the process.
