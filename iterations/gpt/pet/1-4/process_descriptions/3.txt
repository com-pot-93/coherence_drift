The process begins when a decision is made to go public. After this decision, the next step is to select underwriters who will assist with the public offering. Once the underwriters are chosen, a registration statement is prepared. This statement is then submitted for review.

Following the submission, the registration statement is reviewed by the relevant authority. After this review, the underwriters check to ensure that all compliance requirements are met.

At this point, three activities are carried out at the same time:

- The final issue price is determined.
- Preparations are made for a road show to present the offering to potential investors.
- The process waits for approval from the regulatory authority.

Once all three of these activities are completed, the process continues with receiving clearance from the regulatory authority. After clearance is received, the final issue price is set. Then, a firm commitment is made to proceed with the offering. Following this, the stock is offered to the public. The process concludes at this point.
