The process begins when the company decides to go public. After making this decision, the company selects underwriters to assist with the process. Once the underwriters are chosen, the company prepares a registration statement. This registration statement is then submitted for review.

Next, the registration statement is reviewed by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). After the SEC reviews the statement, the underwriters check for compliance with all necessary requirements.

At this point, two activities happen at the same time. On one side, the final issue price is determined, and preparations for the road show are made. On the other side, the process waits for approval from the SEC. Both the preparation for the road show and the wait for SEC approval occur in parallel.

Once both the road show is prepared and SEC approval is received, the process continues. The company receives clearance from the SEC. After receiving this clearance, the final issue price is set. Then, a firm commitment is made to purchase the stock.

Finally, the stock is offered to the public, and the process ends.
