The process begins with an initial step to start the procedure. First, a decision is made about whether to proceed with making the company public. If the decision is to go ahead, the next step is to select underwriters who will help manage the process.

After the underwriters are chosen, a registration statement is prepared. Once this statement is ready, it is submitted for review. The submitted registration statement is then carefully reviewed, and the process continues with a check to ensure all compliance requirements are met.

At this point, three activities are carried out at the same time. One activity involves determining the final issue price for the stock. Another activity is to prepare for a road show, which is an event to present the company to potential investors. The third activity is to wait for regulatory approval.

Once all three of these activities are completed, the process continues with receiving regulatory clearance. After clearance is received, the final issue price is set. Following this, a firm commitment is made to proceed with the offering. The next step is to offer the stock to the public. The process concludes after the stock has been offered.
