The process begins when the company decides to go public. After making this decision, the company selects the underwriters who will manage the public offering. Once chosen, the underwriters prepare a registration statement that must be approved by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

Following the preparation of the registration statement, the underwriters check to ensure that the issue complies with all necessary requirements. At this point, two activities happen at the same time:

- The underwriters begin to determine the final price at which the stock will be offered.
- The underwriters wait for the approval of the registration statement from the SEC.

While the underwriters are working on setting the issue price, they also arrange a road show to present the offering to potential investors. Both the road show and the waiting for SEC approval occur in parallel.

Once both the road show has been arranged and the SEC has approved the registration statement, the process continues. The underwriters then receive official clearance from the SEC. After receiving this clearance, the underwriters set the final issue price for the stock.

Next, the underwriters enter into a firm commitment to purchase the stock from the company. Finally, the underwriters offer the stock to the public, which completes the process.
