The process begins with a starting point. First, a decision is made about whether to go public. Once this decision is made, the next step is to select underwriters who will help with the process. After the underwriters are chosen, a registration statement is prepared.

Following the preparation, the registration statement is submitted. Once submitted, the statement is reviewed. After the review, there is a check to ensure all compliance requirements are met.

At this point, three activities are carried out at the same time. The first activity is to determine the final issue price. The second activity is to prepare for a road show, which is an event to present the company to potential investors. The third activity is to wait for regulatory approval.

Once all three of these activities are completed, the process continues with receiving regulatory clearance. After clearance is received, the final issue price is set. Then, a firm commitment is made to proceed. Following this, the stock is offered to the public. The process ends after the stock is offered.
