The process begins with a starting point. The first step is to start the procedure. After this, a decision is made about whether to proceed with the process or not.

If the decision is not to proceed, the process moves directly to the end.

If the decision is to proceed, the next step is to select underwriters. Once the underwriters are selected, the process continues with preparing the registration statement. After the registration statement is prepared, it is submitted for review. The registration statement is then reviewed, and the compliance requirements are checked.

After checking compliance requirements, three activities are carried out at the same time:

1. The final issue price is determined.
2. Preparations are made for the road show.
3. The process waits for regulatory approval.

Once all three of these activities are completed, the process continues with receiving regulatory clearance. After regulatory clearance is received, the final issue price is set. Then, a firm commitment is made. Following this, the stock is offered to the public.

At this point, another decision is made about whether to proceed. Regardless of the outcome of this decision, the process then comes to an end.
