The process begins with the initiation of the procedure. After this, a decision is made about whether to proceed. If the decision is not to proceed, the process moves directly towards completion without any further steps.

If the decision is to proceed, the next step is to select underwriters. Once the underwriters are selected, a registration statement is prepared. After preparation, the registration statement is submitted. The submitted statement is then reviewed, followed by a check of compliance requirements.

After checking compliance, three activities are carried out at the same time: determining the final issue price, preparing for a road show, and waiting for regulatory approval. These three activities are performed independently and can happen in any order, but all must be completed before moving forward.

Once all three activities are finished, the process continues with receiving regulatory clearance. After receiving clearance, the final issue price is set. Then, a firm commitment is made. Following this, the stock is offered to the public.

Finally, the process concludes. If at any point the decision was made not to proceed after the initial step, the process would have ended immediately.
