The process begins when a guest calls room service. The manager answers the call and records the guest’s order. After the order is recorded, it is submitted as a ticket to the kitchen. The order is then given to the sommelier, and the manager assigns the order to a waiter.

At this point, three activities happen at the same time:  
- The kitchen prepares the order.  
- The sommelier prepares the order.  
- The waiter prepares the cart for delivery.

Once all three preparations are complete, the waiter delivers the cart to the guest. After delivering the cart, the waiter returns to their station.

Next, there is a decision point. The process can go in one of two ways:  
- The waiter may wait before proceeding to billing.  
- Alternatively, the process can move directly to billing, where the guest’s account is debited.

Regardless of the path taken, the process ends after this step.
