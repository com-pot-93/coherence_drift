The process begins. At the start, a decision is made. If the outcome is positive, the company receives an expense report. After receiving the report, another decision is made.

If necessary, the company creates a new employee account. Whether or not a new account is created, the process continues to the next step, where the company reviews the report to determine if it can be approved automatically.

At this point, there are two possible paths:

1. If the report is not approved automatically, a supervisor reviews and approves the report. After the supervisor’s decision, there are two possible outcomes:
   - If the report is approved, the company sends the reimbursement to the employee’s bank account.
   - If the report is rejected, the employee receives a rejection notice.
   After either outcome, the process continues.

2. If the report is approved automatically, the company proceeds with the automatic approval.

After either the supervisor’s approval process or the automatic approval, another decision is made. There are three possible paths from here:

- The company may stop the process. If this happens, the employee receives an email cancellation notice and is prompted to re-submit the expense report. After re-submission, the process returns to the earlier decision point after the company receives the expense report.
- The employee may receive an email stating that approval is in progress. The process then returns to the earlier decision point after the company receives the expense report.
- The process may return directly to the earlier decision point after the company receives the expense report.

If, at the very first decision, the outcome is negative, the process ends.

This cycle continues until the process reaches a conclusion at the first decision point, where the process ends.
