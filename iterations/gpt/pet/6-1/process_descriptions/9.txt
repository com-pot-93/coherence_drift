The process begins when invoices are received. First, the secretariat performs a visual inspection of the invoices. After this inspection, the invoices are forwarded to the accounting department.

In accounting, the supplier is identified and a record is created. Next, the items listed on the invoice are checked. The relevant cost center and managers are then noted. Following this, a docket is prepared and a copy of the invoice is made.

At this point, a decision is made about whether to continue with the process. If the process should not continue, it ends here. If the process continues, the documents are sent to the cost center manager.

Another decision is made regarding the need for the manager’s review. If a review by the manager is required, the manager reviews the documents. After the review, the process returns to the previous decision point to check again if a manager’s review is needed. If no further review is needed, the process moves forward.

Next, the documents are audited by the commercial manager. After the audit, there is a decision about whether a board review is necessary. If a board review is needed, the board reviews the documents, and the process returns to the previous decision point to check again if a board review is needed. If no further board review is required, the process continues.

The docket and the invoice copy are then moved to accounting. At this stage, there is a check for any complaints or issues. If complaints or issues are found, they are resolved, and the process returns to the previous check for complaints or issues. If there are no complaints or issues, payment instructions are prepared and the invoice is closed.

A final check is performed. If further review by the cost center manager is needed, the documents are sent for additional review, and the process returns to the final check. If no further review is required, the process ends.
