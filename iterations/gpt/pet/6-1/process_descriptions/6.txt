The process begins when invoices are received. The following steps are then carried out in order:

1. **Receiving and Initial Handling**
   - The invoices are received.
   - The secretariat performs a visual inspection of the invoices.
   - The invoices are then forwarded to the accounting department.

2. **Accounting Review**
   - The accounting team identifies the supplier and creates a record for the invoice.
   - The items listed on the invoice are checked for accuracy.
   - The relevant cost center and the responsible managers are noted.
   - A docket is prepared, and a copy of the invoice is placed in the internal mail system.

3. **Decision to Continue**
   - A decision is made whether to continue with the process or not.
     - If the process should not continue, the process ends here.
     - If the process continues, the invoice is sent to the cost center manager.

4. **Cost Center Manager Review**
   - The cost center manager receives the invoice.
   - A decision is made whether a review by the manager is needed.
     - If no review is needed, the process moves directly to an audit by the commercial manager.
     - If a review is needed, the process continues as follows:

5. **Manager Review Loop**
   - The manager reviews the invoice.
   - The manager checks for any issues.
     - If issues are found, the invoice is sent back to accounting for clarification.
       - Accounting clarifies the issue with the vendor.
       - The manager reviews the invoice again.
       - If another review is needed, the invoice is sent back to the cost center manager for further review, and this loop can repeat as necessary.
     - If no issues are found, the manager checks if approval is required.
       - If approval is required, the manager writes an approval code.
         - If more managers need to approve, the invoice is passed to the next manager, who repeats the review and approval steps.
         - If all necessary approvals are obtained, the process moves to an audit by the commercial manager.
       - If no approval is needed, the process checks if more managers need to review.
         - If more managers are needed, the invoice is passed to the next manager for review.
         - If all necessary reviews are complete, the process moves to an audit by the commercial manager.

6. **Commercial Manager Audit**
   - The commercial manager audits the invoice.
   - A decision is made whether a review by the board is needed.
     - If a board review is needed, the invoice is sent to the board for review.
       - After the board reviews the invoice, the process continues.
     - If no board review is needed, the process continues.

7. **Final Steps Before Payment**
   - The copy of the invoice and the docket are moved to accounting.
   - The process checks for any complaints or issues.
     - If complaints or issues are found, they are resolved, and the process checks again for further complaints or issues. This can repeat as necessary.
     - If no complaints or issues are found, the process continues.

8. **Payment and Closure**
   - Payment instructions are given, and the invoice is closed.
   - A final check and any remaining steps are performed.
   - A decision is made whether another review by the cost center manager is needed.
     - If another review is needed, the invoice is sent back to the cost center manager for review, and the review loop can repeat as necessary.
     - If no further review is needed, the process continues.

9. **Process Completion**
   - Once all checks, reviews, approvals, audits, and complaint resolutions are complete, and no further actions are required, the process ends.

---

**Key Points:**
- At several points, if issues or complaints are found, the process loops back to earlier steps for clarification, review, or resolution.
- Multiple managers may need to review and/or approve the invoice, and the process supports passing the invoice between managers as needed.
- The commercial manager and, if necessary, the board, perform audits and reviews before payment is finalized.
- The process includes multiple checks for issues or complaints, ensuring that all concerns are addressed before payment is made and the invoice is closed.
- The process ends only when all necessary reviews, approvals, audits, and complaint resolutions are complete, and no further action is required.
