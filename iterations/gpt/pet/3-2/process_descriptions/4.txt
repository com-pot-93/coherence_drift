The process begins with an initial step. After this, the files are checked. Once the files have been checked, a decision is made based on whether the files can be tracked or if any files are missing.

If the files can be tracked, the process continues with tracking the files. If files are missing, a search is conducted to find the missing files. After either tracking the files or searching for missing files, the process moves forward.

At this point, two activities happen at the same time. One activity is handing the files to an associate, and the other is distributing the judge’s lawlist. Both of these activities are carried out in parallel.

Once both handing the files to the associate and distributing the judge’s lawlist are completed, the process continues with conducting directions hearings. After the directions hearings are conducted, the process comes to an end.
