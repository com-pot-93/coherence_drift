The process begins with a starting point. From there, a decision is made that leads to two possible paths.

On the first path, another decision is made, which offers four different options:

- If the first option is chosen, a bill for metering point operations is sent to the grid operator.
- If the second option is chosen, a bill for measurement is sent to the grid operator.
- If the third option is chosen, a bill for additional readings is sent to the grid operator.
- If the fourth option is chosen, a bill for equipment acquisition is sent to the grid operator.

Regardless of which of these four options is selected, the process continues in the same way: the grid operator receives the bill and examines it. After examining the bill, the grid operator makes a decision:

- If the grid operator rejects the invoice, they formally reject it.
- If the grid operator confirms the invoice, they confirm it and provide payment advice.

After either rejecting or confirming the invoice, the process moves to a point where it waits for the other path to finish.

On the second path from the initial decision, a bill for equipment acquisition is sent to the metering point operator network. The metering point operator network then examines the bill and makes a decision:

- If the invoice is confirmed, the metering point operator network confirms it and provides payment advice.
- If the invoice is rejected, the metering point operator network rejects it.

After either confirming or rejecting the invoice, this path also moves to the same waiting point as the first path.

Once both paths have reached this point, the process comes to an end.
