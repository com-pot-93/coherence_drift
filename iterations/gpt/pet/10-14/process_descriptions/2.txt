The process begins with a starting point. Immediately after, there is a decision where the process can follow one of two main paths.

**First Path:**
- The process moves to another decision point, where one of four options is chosen:
  1. A bill for metering point operations is sent to the grid operator.
  2. A bill for measurement is sent to the grid operator.
  3. A bill for additional readings is sent to the grid operator.
  4. A bill for equipment acquisition is sent to the grid operator.
- Regardless of which of these four options is selected, the next step is always the same: the grid operator receives the bill.
- After receiving the bill, the grid operator examines it.
- The grid operator then decides whether to confirm or reject the invoice:
  - If the invoice is rejected, the grid operator rejects the invoice.
  - If the invoice is confirmed, the grid operator confirms the invoice and provides payment advice.
- After either confirming or rejecting the invoice, the process continues to the next stage.

**Second Path:**
- Alternatively, right after the initial decision, the process may proceed to sending a bill for equipment acquisition to the metering point operator network.
- The metering point operator network then examines the bill.
- The metering point operator network decides whether to confirm or reject the invoice:
  - If the invoice is confirmed, the metering point operator network confirms the invoice and provides payment advice.
  - If the invoice is rejected, the metering point operator network rejects the invoice.
- After either confirming or rejecting the invoice, the process continues to the next stage.

**Final Step:**
- After either of the two main paths is completed, the process reaches its end point.
