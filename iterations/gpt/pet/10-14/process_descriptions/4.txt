The process begins with an initial step where the recipient and the type of bill are determined. Based on this decision, the process can follow one of two main paths.

**First Path: Billing the Grid Operator**

If the bill is to be sent to the grid operator, the process continues by identifying the specific type of bill. There are four possible types:

1. **Metering point operations**
2. **Measurement**
3. **Additional readings**
4. **Equipment acquisition**

Depending on the selected type, the corresponding bill is sent to the grid operator. After the bill is sent, the grid operator receives it and then examines its contents. Following this examination, the grid operator makes a decision:

- If the invoice is rejected, the grid operator rejects the invoice.
- If the invoice is confirmed, the grid operator confirms the invoice and provides payment advice.

After either rejecting or confirming the invoice, the process moves towards its conclusion.

**Second Path: Billing the Metering Point Operator Network**

Alternatively, if the bill is to be sent to the metering point operator network, the process proceeds by sending a bill specifically for equipment acquisition to the metering point operator network. The network receives the bill and examines it. After reviewing the bill, the metering point operator network decides:

- If the invoice is rejected, the network rejects the invoice.
- If the invoice is confirmed, the network confirms the invoice and provides payment advice.

After this decision, the process also moves towards its conclusion.

**End of Process**

Regardless of the path taken—whether the bill was sent to the grid operator or the metering point operator network, and whether the invoice was confirmed or rejected—the process ends after these steps are completed.
